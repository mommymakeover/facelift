Liquid Facelift Prices

A liquid facelift combines Botox and injectable fillers, like Juvéderm or Restylane, in a single treatment. The treatment injects hyaluronic acid and Botox to your face where wrinkles, creases and sagging exist. It achieves results that last a few months.

A surgical facelift will achieve results that last longer, however it comes with the risks related to surgery.

The liquid facelift is dependent upon the plastic surgeon administering it. Liquid Facelift combines various products to lift, fill, sculpt and tone the face from the jaw to the hairline.

Using Radiesse: a calcium based product in a gel or Voluma: a Hyaluronic Acid, the mid-face, jawline and cheeks are supported, lifted and smoothed out.

Juvederm is best for lines and volume loss around the mouth and Botox is utilized to balance the forehead eyebrow position and soften the wrinkles. IPL or intense pulsed light diminishes brown spots from sun or age and skin care products refresh and tone the face.

A Combination Of Injectable Fillers

These are all non-surgical and yield very gratifying results. Some are visible in hours and others require a few weeks to fully realize the fullest potential.

Fillers and Botox/Dysport are helpful but don’t take the place of a facelift. The aging process involves 3 things; downward decent from facial soft tissue laxity, skin surface aging characterized by wrinkles and discoloration, and loss of volume especially in the cheek, temple and pre-jowl areas.

The use of fillers or fat transfer will help fill out the skin and may minimize the wrinkles or areas that are hollow and a skin resurfacing procedure such as a TCA peel or laser will tighten your skin However, Failure to treat all three causes of aging may make you more aware of what is not treated: in your case the remaining redundant skin.

A Liquid Facelift Is Not Permanent

The fact that you will not have a facelift, means that there will still be some loose skin despite the other two factors being corrected and may still be a cause for your concern. Also realize that there are alternatives to a full traditional face lift such as a mini lift that you might also consider as it has limited incisions and is less invasive.
Liquid facelift instead of surgery

This is basically using injectable fillers and BOTOX/Dysport to give you a younger appearance, and the procedure involves no surgery. It’s just injections. There are no risks related to surgery. However, results are temporary.

A liquid face lift can have as good results as a face lift depending on your age and skin tone. Fillers like Restylane and liquid injections like Sculptra will build collagen and give you a natural result without surgery.

Sculptra can last two years or longer. Botox in the forehead and Juvederm Voluma in the jowls, and cheeks can provide a lift that can last more than a year and look as good as a face lift in just 20 minutes.

You Can Dramatically Improve Your Appearance

It is really important to find a board certified dermatologist with a good aesthetics and years of experience in facial injections for the best cosmetic results.

A liquid facelift is a marketing term describing a combination of dermal fillers and neuromodulators (such as Botox) that are used to correct signs of aging and enhance the youthfulness of the face.

While the results of a liquid facelift are not comparable to the results of a surgical facelift, liquid facelifts can smooth wrinkles and replace lost volume that contribute to the aging face.

Injectable Products Can Correct Asymmetries

Liquid facelifts have much less risk and downtime than a surgical facelift. Liquid facelifts are great options for patients who are not ready to undergo surgery and who do not yet have severe signs of aging.
