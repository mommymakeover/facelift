What is a facelift?

Facelift in Thailand is a plastic surgery aimed at eliminating the natural signs of aging in the face and neck area. With this operation it is possible to reduce nasolabial folds, smooth wrinkles on the cheeks and neck, tighten the skin, restore the contour of the face, make the chin line clearer, get rid of the “bulldog cheeks”.

Before And After Facelift Without Surgery Thailand

It must be remembered that the circular facelift does not affect the natural processes of skin aging, it only reduces their visible manifestations. The operation is performed for women and men.

Indications for facelift.

  • vertical wrinkles on the cheeks
  • pronounced, deep nasolabial folds
  • flabbiness, sagging of the skin on the face and neck
  • fuzzy, “floated” outline of the face

Contraindications to facelift

  • infectious diseases
  • oncological diseases
  • blood clotting disorders
  • diabetes mellitus in the stage of decompensation
  • cardiovascular diseases

Facelift – when and how to operate?

The maximum effect after surgery in Thailand can be expected if the face lift is carried out at a certain age. The most common age group is people 50 to 60 years old.

Before the operation, the doctor takes into account not only the age, but the structure and elasticity of the patient’s skin. A facelift should be performed when the sagging of the skin on the neck and face (aptosis) has already become very noticeable.

With age, the elasticity of the tissue is dramatically reduced, so often very noticeable sagging of the skin occurs in the lower third of the face, ie, At the level of the lower jaw and in the neck under the chin.

The effect after the operation is maintained for 8-10 years. If a second operation to carry out in 3 – 4 years, it is impossible to exclude the effect of “mask-like” face. The risk of keloid, stretched scars and thinned skin increases.

The appearance of signs of skin aging, its sagging and the formation of wrinkles depends on the individual characteristics of the person. Therefore, during facelift operation, it is necessary to concentrate separately on each face area.

Sometimes it is necessary to pay more attention to the area of the neck and the line of the chin. In another case, you should first concentrate on the area of the cheeks and temples, etc.

Also, the presence and depth of wrinkles is an individual factor. We must understand that wrinkles are just a reflection of our habitual facial expressions and the ability of the skin to respond to the movement of facial muscles.

Circular facelift tightens the skin of the face and neck, allowing you to adjust the facial wrinkles. However, during facelift, the facial muscles are not affected, and therefore, in the course of time, mimic wrinkles may appear again.

Nasolabial folds from the wings of the nose to the corners of the mouth and forming a natural border between the group of buccal muscles and the circular muscle of the mouth are present on the face always.

Facelift in Thailand helps to reduce them, but can never completely smooth them out. This also applies to small vertical wrinkles above the upper and lower lip.

We can say that a person who has a narrow, not full face and thin skin, the effect after the operation will be better than a person who has a round, full face with a large enough fat layer.

How is surgery performed?

  1. The operation is performed under general anesthesia or in analgesia.
  2. After preoperative marking and thorough disinfection of the face and hair, a skin incision is made that begins in the hair of the temporal region, bends around the ear (in some cases passes behind the goat), then goes around the lobe and ends behind the ear (towards the scalp).
  3. In the next stage, the skin of the face and neck is detachment, tightened and fixed in a new position. Excess skin is excised. Depending on the characteristics of ptosis, SMAS facelift is carried out simultaneously with classical facelift. This intervention in deep subcutaneous structures, when during the operation not only the skin but also the muscle tissues are moved.

  4. At the end of the operation, the seams are superimposed on the surgical incisions. Internal seams – absorbable. External seams can be both absorbable and non-absorbable. In most cases, thin drainage is established to drain blood for 24 hours after the operation.
  5. The operation ends with the application of a tight bandage around the head. As a rule, the operation lasts 1.5 – 3 hours.
  6. After the operation, the client is in hospital for two days. For the next 10-14 days, it is recommended that you follow a gentle regimen.

Recovery period

  • After the operation, the patient is in the hospital for two days. On the second day, drainage is removed, the first dressing is performed and a special compression bandage is put on the head.
    Sleep is necessary on his back with head elevated.
  • In the first days after the operation, edema of the face and eyelids is observed, bruising can occur, the temperature can rise to 38. These are normal postoperative events. Therefore, especially in the first 4-5 days it is recommended to observe a sparing regimen, do not sharply turn your head, exclude physical exertion, apply cold compresses on your face and eyelids. It is necessary to take prescribed antibiotics and medications to reduce swelling.

  • As a rule, postoperative pain manifestations are minimal. Compression bandage and limited head movement can create unpleasant sensations.
    Wash your head in 4 to 5 days. To wash your head, use an ordinary shampoo, do not dry your hair with a hairdryer. The next 4 days, wash your hair regularly every day, near the seams – very carefully.
  • After 4-5 days, after the first washing of the head, you can stop constantly wearing a compression bandage. At night, it is recommended to tie a headscarf on your head or dress a bandage.
  • Suture removal (if the seams were not absorbable) occurs 10 to 14 days later. Swelling and bruising are very individual. Gentle mode is recommended to be observed within 14 days. Physical exercise is possible one month after the operation.
  • After 3 weeks after the operation, start to do pressing massages, at least three times a day for 15 minutes. During the discharge from the clinic, the doctor will give instructions on how to properly massage.

  • The healing and appearance of scars are very individual factors. After removing the scars stitches are pink, but gradually fade seams. The longest pink color remains in the outer corners of the seam on the upper eyelids. In these places, the stitches turn pale for several weeks.
