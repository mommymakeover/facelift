I believe I have a tubular breast. Is my goal photo achievable?
Correction of asymmetric, ptotic, tubular breasts
Jan 1, 2020
From examining your photo, it appears to me that the right breast just needs a lift with maybe a small implant. The left breast is more difficult since it is smaller in volume, has more ptosis, a larger areola/nipple complex, and a more constricted or tubular appearance. This breast will also need a higher lift and a little bigger implant. Achieving symmetry will be difficult for the inexperienced surgeon.

Is Mommy Makeover safe with Rheumatoid Arthritis ?
Dec 29, 2019
I have performed dozens of cosmetic surgeries on patients with Rheumatoid Arthritis and other autoimmune diseases. You need get medical clearance from your Rheumatologist and certain medications will need to be stopped before and after surgery to insure normal wound healing. Silicone breast implants are not FDA approved for patients with autoimmune disease, but from your photograph, it doesn't appear you need implants.
