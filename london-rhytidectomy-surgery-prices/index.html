In the case of strong facial aging, facelift is a very effective solution. Thanks to facelift, you can noticeably become younger for several years, and this method is suitable for both men and women.

What is a facelift?

Facelift in London is an aesthetic operation of the face, in which the skin, connective tissue is lifted, if necessary – the removal of excess fatty tissue.

Very often, along with this operation, the neck is rejuvenated. Facelift can be combined with other operations on the face. Most often it is blepharoplasty.

Preparation for facelift.

Before making a decision about facelift, a careful consultation with an aesthetic surgeon is necessary, which will assess the skin condition and its elasticity. In the event that the aging process has not left significant marks on the face, the doctor can recommend another, less complicated alternative to rejuvenating the exterior. With regard to any unclear questions and doubts in making a decision, it is necessary to consult a doctor.

It is also necessary to be prepared for the fact that in the first days after the operation the face will be swollen and with bruises. At first it can cause a feeling of disappointment. It is necessary for the patient to know that his face will quickly heal and only after that it will be possible to evaluate the result. So you can avoid unnecessary mental unrest.

Due to the fact that this is a rather complicated operation, a preoperative examination by a therapist is required. During the operation, the patient should not be ill with any acute infectious disease, including there should not be even a common cold.

It must be remembered that although this is an aesthetic operation, it is still a surgical intervention. And in the case of the best preparation and care, a certain risk is associated with it. This risk can be reduced to a minimum by careful preparation.

Dr Amir Nakhdjevani SMAS Facelift

If the patient is taking medications that increase bleeding, he should inform the doctor about this, who obviously advises them to stop taking them for some time before the operation. In the event that the patient showed a tendency to excessive scarring in the past, he probably should not suffer an aesthetic operation. Its result will not be favorable.

Facelift in London usually lasts 1.5 – 3 hours. It is carried out, or with general anesthesia or analgosedation. The surgeon will first free the skin in the face and neck area from the underlying structures, and after accurately measuring the excesses will perform their removal. As part of the operation, the tightening of the connective tissue apparatus is performed.

The surgeon will also remove excess fat deposits, for example, in the area of the double chin. The surgeon makes incisions near the hairline to the rumen, which is formed later, was covered with hair.

Absorbable sutures are commonly used to close the surgical wound. Sometimes drains are introduced, through which the secret and blood are removed from the wound. The operable surface is sterilely closed and fixed with an elastic bandage.

After a facelift

Immediately after the intervention, there are no painful sensations. The patient can take analgesics. The face is considerably swollen, with minor bruises, which can cause anxiety. These symptoms quickly disappear, and as the result of the operation is seen, the patient will be satisfied. You can go home as a rule on the second day after the operation, then in the majority of cases, drains are also removed. Bandages are removed after 3 to 5 days. The period of incapacity for work is 1-2 weeks, depending on the type of profession.

The result of facelift

The determined result of the operation is visible even after the wound is fully healed, when the edema subsides. The duration of the effect is very individual. Usually it is about 5 – 10 years.

It is important to remember that despite the fact that the operation will bring with it a visible rejuvenation, it will not prevent the aging process. The operation can be repeated as necessary.

Complications after facelift

A facelift in London GB can be accompanied by common complications like every other surgery associated with surgical intervention. This is mainly increased bleeding from the wound, infection. Temporarily, there may be a loss of sensitivity in the operated area. In case of increased soreness, bleeding, or secretion from the wound, and if there is a fever or other unusual signs, contact a doctor who will do everything necessary to effectively combat complications.
