How Much Does A Facelift Cost In Montreal?

Facelift in Montreal is a plastic surgery and a set of accompanying preparatory and restorative measures aimed at cardinal rejuvenation of the appearance, which has deteriorated due to the gravitational ptosis of tissues and the formation of excessive fat deposits caused by age-related changes.

The operation is performed under general anesthesia, its results are manifested for several months, and success depends on the practical art and aesthetic taste of the plastic surgeon. Facelift is a complex surgical procedure that directly or indirectly affects all facial features on the face: a new shape can be found not only by the oval of the face, chin, forehead, but also the neck, lips, nose, nasolabial folds, infraorbital fissures, eye section.

Dr Carlos Cordoba SMAS Facelift

All this is preliminarily negotiated with the patient and modeled by the surgeon in harmonious proportions, therefore the degree of influence on each of these organs in the individual case will be different.

Depending on the upcoming changes and the volume of excess tissues, the specialist decides on the method of carrying out the operation: facelift can be performed both by classical surgery (tissue dissection) and endoscopic (through punctures) methods.

Advantages of Facelift surgery

Facelift in Montreal allows to achieve the most impressive visual effect with the highest duration, in comparison with any other procedures of rejuvenation. With the help of this method, you can externally look younger for 10-20 years and keep the results achieved with simple cosmetology procedures for 10 years or more.

The sagging cheeks, the “second” chin, the lowering of the corners of the lips and eyes, the hanging eyebrows, wrinkles on the forehead, deep nasolabial folds and infraorbital furrows and many other age-related problems of appearance are solved by this method immediately and for a long time.

Indications and contraindications.

Facelift surgery is indicated to patients to solve the following problems:

  • Age-related changes in appearance: gravitational ptosis (sagging) of the cheeks, chin, deep nasolabial and nasolacrimal furrows, wrinkles and creases on the forehead, nose, corners of the lips and eyes,
  • Presence of congenital or acquired defects of appearance,
  • Asymmetry of the face,
  • Disproportion of appearance, categorically not satisfying the patient.

Like any plastic surgery, a facelift surgery also has its own contraindications:

  • Age of the patient up to 30 years (with correction of age changes),
  • Inflammatory processes,
  • oncological diseases,
  • Violations of blood clotting or taking drugs that reduce it.

By the method of surgical intervention facelift is divided into classical and endoscopic. Each of these methods has its own additional indications and contraindications; In addition, they differ in the tolerability, depth of impact and duration of the effect achieved. However, in both cases, inhalation anesthesia is used to perform the operation.

As additional elements of such an operation, implants can be used in the chin area, SPRS-therapy (skin rejuvenation with the help of the patient’s own fibroblasts) and so on, according to individual indications.

How is the facelift carried out?

Surgical facelift in Montreal is an operation under general anesthesia lasting an average of about two hours. The skin, the fatty body of the cheek, the muscles of the face and the shell of the muscles are subjected to the surgeon’s intervention. During the operation, all these layers can undergo selection, movement and fixation in a new position.

After the operation, it is necessary to observe in the hospital for at least three days. Suture removal is carried out on average a week. The sick leave is given for two weeks, during which the patient will have to wear a fixing bandage.

Restriction of physical activity is extended for up to one month. The patient will be able to appreciate the results achieved at the end of this period.

Contour Correction of face. FAQ.

What is a contour correction procedure?

The procedure of contour correction is aimed at filling soft tissue volumes in the area of nasolabial furrows, in the region of the cheeks, chin, restoration of the facial contours.

To do this, most often used hyaluronic acid, which are able to stay in the tissues for a long time (up to 1-1,5 years) and fill the volume in the places where it is necessary.

Is it possible to rejuvenate the face only with the procedures of contour correction and not to resort to surgical intervention?

It is impossible to fully cope with the age-related skin changes, without the use of surgical correction. Because with age there is a stretching of the skin occurs besides its qualitative change.

Is it necessary to constantly make injections of hyaluronic acid, and will not the face look worse after complete resolution of the drug?

After complete resorption of the hyaluronic acid after 1-1,5 years, if you do not carry out correction, the face returns to the original condition. Overstretch of tissues, as many patients think, does not occur. Because during contour correction, the physician replenishes the lost volume where it was before.
