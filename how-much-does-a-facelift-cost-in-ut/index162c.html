How Much Does A Facelift Cost In Utah?

So-called facelift is performed to tighten and restore clear contours of the face and neck, eliminate wrinkles, and to return of elasticity the skin for a long period of time.

The operation performed at a high professional level enables the patient to have a second youth and increase self-esteem.

This rejuvenating operation in the field of plastic surgery is also called rhytidectomy. The duration of the result is directly dependent on the state of health and specific skin characteristics, as well as on the technique used for the operation.

This period ranges from seven to twelve years on average. Facelift is performed as completely (lifting the forehead, neck, temples, cheeks, chin), and in a certain area of the face.

Before And After Liquid Facelift In Utah

Facelift in Utah allows the patient to get rid of the sagging of the eyebrows, the lowering of the corners of the mouth, the sagging of the soft tissues in the cheek, neck and chin area (the second chin), and adjust the facial contours.

In addition, this plastic surgery allows to eliminate the severity of age-related changes, remove existing wrinkles, folds (including nasolabial), pigmentation, thereby achieving significant rejuvenation of the face.

The history of the development of methods of facelift.

A facelift of the first generation, expressed in minimal subcutaneous mobilization and excision of skin excess, began to be applied in the first decade of the twentieth century.
Facelift of the second generation or Smas facelift is a more modern concept of rhytidectomy, which dates back to 1970 of the last century.

Features of the procedure

One of the newest and increasingly popular methods of facelift is endoscopic facelift in Utah.

The operation is carried out by means small incisions that the surgeon does in inaccessible areas outside view, in particular in the area of natural hair growth and skin folds. All this is achieved through the use of special tools and endoscopic equipment.

When preparing for facelift, the patient undergoes a full medical examination, the existing contraindications are revealed. If the patient is completely healthy, the date of the operation is appointed and the conditions of the preparatory phase are negotiated.

Rhytidectomy With Dr Angela Keen

Immediately before the operation, the marking of the zones of the passage of the branches of the facial nerve is carried out, lines of incisions are noted. Hair cut in this case is not required, simply carried out a special styling.

The operation is performed using general anesthesia. The operation lasts an average of one to three hours, depending on the amount of tasks (in rare cases, the duration may be four hours). The mini-scar is hardly noticeable that remain after surgery in places of incisions.

The duration of the postoperative period is approximately three weeks, the first two days the patient is in hospital under the supervision of specialists.

After about five days, the first dressing is done, the seams are removed on the seventh day. Observed after surgery swelling subsides after about two to three weeks. A full face recovery takes place over a period of one and a half to two months. At this time, the patient is recommended to abandon any physical exertion.

What is the best age for facelift?

The optimal age for facelift is the period from forty to sixty years of age, at a time when the signs of aging have become pronounced, but the skin has not lost its elasticity yet, and the facial bones have not yet undergone age-related changes, which allows achieving an ideal contour and restoring the elasticity of muscles. In addition, the physical condition of the patient during this period does not cause strong concerns among specialists.

At a more mature age, this facial rejuvenation surgery will have less pronounced effect and less lasting result, as the elasticity of the patient’s skin will be practically lost.

Preparing for a facelift

If there were no contraindications to the facelift during the examination, then two weeks before the appointed date, the patient should stop smoking, stop taking hormonal medications, drugs that affect blood coagulability, as well as any other drugs or antitussives. Immediately on the day of the facelift, the patient is not allowed to take water and food.

Vampire Facelift Utah

After the operation, the patient must strictly follow all the recommendations and prescriptions of the doctor, which will shorten the duration of the rehabilitation period and facilitate its course.

Contraindications for the operation:

  • Diseases of internal organs of a serious nature;
  • Presence of oncological diseases;
  • Presence of infectious diseases;
  • Various blood diseases, in particular a clotting disorder;
  • Cardiovascular diseases in the stage of decompensation;
  • The presence of chronic diseases in the stage of exacerbation;
  • Severe hypertension.

Recovery after surgery

After the operation, there may be some decrease in the sensitivity of the skin in the zone of the temples, forehead and scalp. The patient returns to the usual way of life already on the seventh-twelfth day. It is possible to fully evaluate the result of a facelift after one and a half to two months.

Possible complications:

  • hematoma;
  • Damage to the nerve branches responsible for the movement of the facial muscles;
  • Slow wound healing.
