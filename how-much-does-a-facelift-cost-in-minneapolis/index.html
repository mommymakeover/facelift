Complications after facelift

Do not forget that facelift is a surgical operation, therefore, after it is carried out, there is always a risk of developing various complications. The patient should observe a certain mode of life, nutrition before and after the operation in order to minimize the risk of complications to a minimum.

The most typical complications of facelift:

1. Postoperative bleeding

If after an intervention the swelling of the face increases, pain increases, then this often indicates the occurrence of bleeding. It can appear in the first ten hours after the operation.

At this time the patient is still in the clinic, therefore such consequences are easily solved on the spot: the surgeon inspects the places of the incisions, determines where the problem arose, opens access, removes blood and cauterizes the blood vessels.

2. Hematomas and swelling

It is important to determine the appearance of hematomas in the early stages. After the operation, the doctor performs a thorough examination, and if there are places where the gore is accumulated, it is better to clean them. Otherwise, late diagnosis is fraught with skin necrosis. Small hematomas dissolve by themselves gradually. The same happens with swelling.

Skin necrosis

Smokers become frequent victims of such complications. Their tissues are most vulnerable and are subject to withering away. That is why it is strongly recommended to quit smoking at least two weeks before the operation. Necrosis of the skin and tissue will not occur if all the prescriptions of the doctor are followed and a postoperative examination is performed. The risk of this type of complication in healthy patients is practically reduced to zero.

Suppuration of wounds

This complication occurs when infection and non-compliance with strict hygiene rules. Occasionally, suppuration occurs due to hematoma or because of hairs in the wounds.

The formation of scar tissue

Most often, hypertrophic scars appear in patients with a special skin structure, which is prone to the formation of this type of tissue. As a rule, pre-operative examination helps the surgeon to take into account the type of skin and avoid such a complication by choosing the method of suturing.

Skin pigmentation

In some patients after facelift, certain areas of the skin become lighter. This phenomenon is a sign of small hemorrhages and features of sensitive skin. Pigmented spots disappear within a year after the operation.

Deformation of the face contour

This complication occurs with inaccurate planning of the operation, the appearance of bruising, too much tension on the skin. Most often, this trouble can be avoided, if you choose an experienced surgeon, who will take into account all the features and will conduct the operation professionally and qualitatively.

Hair loss

Hair can fall out in places of cuts because of disturbed growth of hair bulbs. Usually the hairline is restored gradually, for several months.

Complications after surgery will be minimized if the patient is to monitor his health and does not neglect the recommendations and prescriptions of the doctor.
