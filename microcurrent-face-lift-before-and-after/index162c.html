Facial microcurrent treatments and a surgical facelift are two extremely different types of procedures that do not yield even remotely similar results. A facelift can successfully restore the loss of volume back to the face, lift sagging facial tissues and remove excess skin and tissue from the face resulting in a more youthful, rejuvenated facial appearance.
Facial microcurrent treatments are based on the theory that the skin can be rejuvenated by utilizing electrical stimulation. There are claims that facial microcurrent treatments can heal soft tissue and bones, tighten skin tissue and improve skin elasticity.

However, there is not any solid data to date to support any of these claims. If you do choose facial microcurrent treatment as an option it should not interfere with any filler procedures but it is best to follow the advice of the provider. (Scott Chapin, MD, FACS, Philadelphia Plastic Surgeon)
