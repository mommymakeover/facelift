Facelift surgery, also known as rhytidectomy, is a cosmetic surgery procedure that aims to reduce, if not totally eliminate, wrinkles and facial lines.

These wrinkles and lines can make you look tired and old – perhaps even older than your actual age. While aging is the main culprit, other factors such as sun exposure, gravity and stress hasten the development of these wrinkles and lines as well.

Facelift surgery is also a cosmetic remedy against these skin issues that can add years to your face: slackening jaw line, creases appearing near the mouth, and the presence of folds and fat deposits on the neck. Furthermore, facelift tightens sagging skin and lifts areas of the face and neck that are significantly losing their elasticity.

If you are interested in looking younger and revitalized, then facelift surgery is definitely an option for you.

If you are interested in learning more about facelift surgery, here are some answers to a few frequently asked questions:

Who should receive facelift surgery?

Anybody who is interested in achieving a younger and revitalized facial skin can undergo facelift surgery. As time goes by, solar exposure, gravity, other nature elements and stress can take their toll on your face. This can cause slackening of the jaw line, deep creases to appear beside the mouth and the appearance of folds and fat deposits on the neck. If you feel that you are starting to notice these facial skin maladies, do not hesitate to schedule a consultation with Dr.

What are the expected results of a facelift?

The ultimate goal of facelift surgery in Naples is to restore the smooth, youthful and exuberant appearance of your face. It will reduce the visibility of facial lines and wrinkles, tighten sagging skin, and lift areas of the face and neck that are suddenly losing elasticity.

Doctor Richard W. Maloney Blepharoplasty

Is a hospital stay required after the procedure?

Generally, there is no need for a hospital stay. Facelift procedures are typically performed either as an outpatient basis at an ambulatory surgical care facility or in the hospital. After surgery, you can normally go home that same day. Occasionally, however, an overnight stay is recommended by Dr.

Is any anesthesia used during surgery?

Yes. Rhytidectomy procedures are normally performed under general anesthesia, which is administered by an expert anesthesiologist.

How long is the surgery?

Dr. usually takes several hours, depending on the extent of your facial condition and necessary cosmetic changes, to complete a facelift surgery.

How is the actual facelift performed?

Unless considerable skin laxity exists, Dr. will normally perform a short scar mini-facelift for most patients. He prefers to make incisions that are well hidden. The incision starts from the hairline, continues to a natural crease in front of the ear, inside the ear cartilage, and ends around the ear lobule behind the ear as needed. If a neck lift is necessary, a small incision may be made under the chin.

Dr. Elizabeth Fox What’s A Facelift

After that, Dr. will then elevate the facial skin from the underlying fat and muscles. The underlying muscle fascia is then tightened and the fat elevated to precisely correct the anatomical changes of the aged face. Lastly, the excess skin is then excised.

Can facelift surgery be performed in conjunction with other cosmetic procedures?

To provide full facial rejuvenation, a facelift is often performed in conjunction with a brow lift. Eyelid surgery (blepharoplasty) may also be performed at the same time as the facelift, especially if a patient has significant overhanging skin in the upper eyelids. It is best to consult thoroughly with Dr. regarding all your concerns and cosmetic goals.
