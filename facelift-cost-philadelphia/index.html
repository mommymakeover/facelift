Facelift Cost Philadelphia

Facelift in Philadelphia is a plastic operation that is performed by circular tightening of the soft tissues of the face, tightening the soft tissues of the lower 2/3 faces.

This is one of the most effective and efficient ways to eliminate signs of aging. The optimal age of the patient for this procedure is 40-60 years.

Facelift is performed to correct nasolabial folds, creases on the cheeks; Elimination of “double chin”, flew.

Endoscopic facelift is a tightening of the soft tissues of the upper parts of the face. It is carried out with the purpose of elimination of wrinkles of forehead and raising of eyebrows; Omitted outer corners of the eyes; Ptosis of the patient’s soft tissues.

Facelift in stages

Facelift in Philadelphia is divided into cutaneous facelift and skin-muscular by the way it is performed. Skin incision for a facelift are on the scalp in the temporal and occipital areas, and in the area of the ear – at the leading edge of the latter with the rounding of the ear lobe and the transition to BTE, and then the occipital region.

The essence of facelift is the separation of the facial skin from the underlying tissues, its tightening, fixation in a new position and excision of the excess skin formed.

When cutaneous facelift produces detachment and stretching with the fixation in the new position in addition to the skin and even the so-called superficial musculo-aponeurotic system face (SMAS). SMAS facelift is more preferable in terms of the duration and stability of the achieved result, but accordingly complicates the operation technically, increases the time of intervention and recovery period. The sometimes observed decrease in the sensitivity of individual skin areas, that disappears within 2-3 months.

  • Anesthesia. In view of the duration of the operation, a general anesthesia is preferred.

The recovery period after facelift:

In the postoperative period, swelling and bruising are observed in all patients – it is recommended to use medicines, special ointments and physical procedures, which shortens the rehabilitation period to 2-3 weeks. In the first 4-5 days, a compression mask is prescribed.

After the procedure, it is necessary to follow all the recommendations of the doctor, including the need to exclude physical activity, slopes, lifting weights for three weeks; It is necessary to exclude thermal procedures, hot baths, saunas, not to go to countries with a hot climate for four weeks.
