Is there a surgery to fix my uneven eyes?
eyeball position
Feb 2, 2022
It appears that your actual eyeballs are sitting in different positions. You right eye may be sitting lower (hypoglobus) or your left eye may be sitting high (hyperglobus). Frequently this is a physiologic condition that people are born with. However, there are a number of other causes that need to be ruled out before determining which treatment would be the correct one for you. I recommend that you see an Oculoplastic surgeon who has experience in orbital diseases.

Removing fat from outer corner of eyes – could a lateral blepharoplasty be effective?
Treatment of misplaced fat grafting
Nov 1, 2021
It appears that you have a small amount of grafted fat in the lateral orbicularis (which is circled). We typically do not have any subcutaneous fat in this area. Your surgeon could easily aspirate the grafted fat with a fine cannula or a lateral blepharoplasty (incision along the natural lid crease) could be made and the fat removed. If you have not tried gently massaging that area daily, it may be worthwhile to see it the “lump” will flatten.

Sudden change of eyelid – what could be the cause?
Cause of swelling
Oct 15, 2021
It is very hard to tell from your photos. It could be allergy related, mechanical irritation (ie rubbing), focal inhalation (like a stye of chalazion) or just worsening ptosis. You could try Upneeq, which is an eye drop that will temporarily raise the affected eyelid.
With new onset, I would want a further work up to rule out other causes of acute onset ptosis, like cranial nerve palsies, myasthenia graves, thyroids issues.
