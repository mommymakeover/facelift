No matter how sad it may be, but people, like all living things, inevitably grows old, and it is the result of perfectly natural general biological changes that must take place in any living organism.

The aging of the face is inevitable, including its soft tissues, which leads to the formation of wrinkles, the appearance of nasolabial folds, to the sagging of the cheeks, to the formation of fatty deposits.

As a result of many years of observations, cosmetologists and plastic surgeons say that the anatomical structure of the skin, the anatomy of the fatty body of the cheek (the so-called Bishka lump), and the SMAS system are changing in the first place.

What is the basis of age-related changes in the face?

Cosmetic Surgical Face Lift With Dr. James M. Pearson

Researchers say with confidence: any changes that occur with age (the so-called age-related changes) are explained by the fact that in the soft tissues of the face the amount of collagen structures decreases.

In addition, the forces of gravity act on soft tissues, and as collagen support weakens with age, the cheeks, eyelids and eyebrows begin to sag more and more. Then, the formation of the infraorbital and nasolabial furrows begins, and the neck is also affected: the cervical-chin angle disappears, the formation of horizontal wrinkles begins, and the skin becomes inelastic, lifeless and flabby.

It’s not a secret for anyone that aging, including aging of the face (its soft tissues) is accelerated by the wrong way of life, the presence of any diseases of various organs and systems of the body, the constant impact of various harmful factors, including environmental factors, and any bad habits.

It is this complex of causes that is formed individually for each individual, leads to pronounced signs of aging (a change in the face oval, the formation of facial wrinkles in the forehead and eyes, the appearance of nasolabial and infraorbital furrows), when cosmetic procedures become powerless and ineffective.

If we talk about surgical facelift, then, probably, many people will be interested in that, such operations began to be performed more than a hundred years ago. For about the first time a surgical procedure for correction of age-induced changes in the soft tissues of the face were reported in 1906.
In two decades, in 1926, the procedure for removal (excision) of excess skin in the temporal region and in the eye area was described in detail.

With the development of medicine, pharmaceuticals, medical equipment, the development of plastic surgery continued, including facelifts. However, it turned out, that for better surgical intervention is necessary to correct not only the skin of the face, but also perform the correction of the myofascial complex. The technique of such facelift operations was described in detail in 1974.

Dr Justin Yovino Plastic Surgery Jowls With SMAS

A little later, in 1976, a detailed description of the muscular-aponeurotic layer was made and the term SMAS (subcutaneous muscle-aponeurotic system), which is now widely used by plastic surgeons around the world. However, the methods of resection and fixation of SMAS were developed somewhat later and were described only in 1982.

In 1979, the movement of composite (multicomponent) flaps was finalized and described in detail, and surgical techniques for tightening the skin of the forehead and the middle third of the face were improved.

Of course, research in the field of plastic surgery did not end and successfully continues to this day. However, these were exactly the researches and discoveries that made it possible to perform a plastic surgery as effectively and practically safe. It is very important that modern plastic surgery uses techniques that allow you to adjust any problem area of the face separately.
