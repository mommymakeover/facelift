Why would a Surgeon choose a SMAS-imbrication over a SMAS-ectomy, where is the logic in keeping excess
How to operate on SMAS during facelift
Jun 4, 2020
Your question assumes a "one size fits all approach to surgery". Patients with fat faces due to thick skin and deeper tissues will benefit from techniques that remove SMAS. Patients who are very thin need to save all the subcutaneous tissue possible so they won't look like walking skeletons. Techniques that excise or spare the SMAS can both achieve the same degree of repositioning.
