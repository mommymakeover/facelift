A facelift in Sacramento is a surgical procedure designed to remove excess fat and skin, as well as tighten the muscles of the face and neck. A facelift is often performed in conjunction with another cosmetic procedure such as blepharoplasty or microdermabrasion.

The Surgery

Facelift surgery is generally performed on an outpatient basis under local anesthesia combined with a sedative. The surgeon begins by making an incision at the temples just above the hairline.

The incision continues down in front of the ear, around the earlobe, and to the lower scalp. If any work is to be done on the neck, a small incision will be placed under the chin.

The next step will be to separate the skin from the underlying fat and muscle. Fat will be suctioned or trimmed around the neck and chin, tissue may be repositioned, and the muscles will be tightened. The skin is then tightened and any excess is removed. Stitches are used to secure the facial tissue and close the incisions and, in some cases, metal clips will be used on the scalp.

Finally, a small tube may be temporarily placed under the skin for blood drainage and the surgeon will wrap the patient’s head with bandages to reduce bruising and swelling. The entire surgery will take several hours, maybe longer if it is combined with other procedures.

Facelift Risks

As with any surgery, there are risks associated with a facelift. Common complications with all surgeries include:

  • Infection
  • Bleeding
  • Reaction to the anesthesia
  • Hematoma – a collection of blood under the skin that must be surgically removed

Risks specific to facelift surgery include:

  • Injury to the nerves that control facial muscles – this is usually temporary
  • Problems with healing of the skin – this is common for patients who smoke
  • Scarring – there will be some scarring, although the surgeon will try to hide the scars
