Lollipop or anchor lift?
Breast lift options
Apr 24, 2018
Thanks for your question.  A traditional anchor breast lift will remove excess skin, but often the breast will settle back to a low position.  There are some breast lift techniques that use natural breast structure to restore volume to the upper breast and eliminate most of the scar under the breast.  One such technique is the structural breast lift, which we have used successfully in many patients with your general breast shape.  Best of luck.
Do I have tuberous breasts? To improve shape and fullness, what would you recommend?
Tuberous Breasts versus Congenital Breast Ptosis
Aug 11, 2017
Thanks for your question.  From your pictures it does not appear that you have tuberous breasts.  Some people have breasts that are ptotic (which simply means settled or droopy) ever since their breasts begin to develop.  The good news is that your breasts should respond well to a breast lift.  There are many types of breast lifts. Some just tighten skin; others rearrange breast structure and anchor the breast at deeper levels.  Best of luck.
Is their anything else besides fillers to fill my cheeks up? Will a mini facelift work?
Strategies for volume loss in the cheek
Aug 11, 2017
Thanks for your question.  For most patients the best answer for volume loss of the cheeks is a fat transfer.  This lasts longer than fillers and provides benefits to the overlying skin.  A cheek lift will not restore volume, so it is important to make the proper diagnosis and determine if the main issue is structural descent or volume loss.  Best of luck.

Revision surgery for breast asymmetry
Aug 11, 2017
Thanks for your question.  From your picture it appears there could be several possible explanations for the differences between your breasts.  Part of the difference may be that there was an asymmetry of breast contour before the surgery.  If one breast had a greater degree of overhang than the other before you had your surgery, this can be very difficult to correct with implants alone.  It all comes down to a detailed physical exam and thorough discussion with your surgeon.  Best of luck
Can I wear a band 4.6 months post op to help my breasts drop?
Bands for high-riding Implants
Aug 11, 2017
