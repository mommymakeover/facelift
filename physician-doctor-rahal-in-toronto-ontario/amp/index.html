11 months post 4000 grafts FUE, what can be done to thicken my hair?
11 months post 4000 grafts FUE
Aug 7, 2018
The area transplanted was quite large and will not create the desired density. Plus, some (many) of the grafts did not grow and thus giving the light coverage look. Doing 4000 grafts in one setting with curly hair is very challenging and if done poorly or rushed could lead to negative consequences.
Was this procedure done in one day or two? Was it a sharp or semi sharp punch? Did the extractor physician have experience with curly hair?
Should only single hair grafts be used in the front of the hairline? 5 months post-op.
single hair grafts – natural hairline
Aug 7, 2018
The hairline should be done in 100% single hairs in the proper angle and direction in order to get natural result. Anything more than single hair, esp if have coarser hair, and you will get an unnatural outcome that looks pluggy.
The triples and double can be removed by FUE and reused behind the hairline. Unfortunately many clinics that don’t use microscope during cutting and magnification during placing of grafts will end up using doubles and triples instead of singles.

How can a 2nd FUT make my first FUT scar smaller? How can a scar but cut and removed?
Can a 2nd FUT make my first FUT scar smaller
Aug 7, 2018
It is possible to make a scar smaller assuming few things. That:
– the first scar is so wide that it can be improved,
-the laxity of skin is still good to be mobilised
-also assuming first surgery/scar was done with poor technique and that the new surgery (assuming better technique) will improve it
-that you are just removing the scar and not attempting to remove as strip and scar
Even with all those factors, the scar may still heal the same size, smaller or even bigger…
I read that a regular tattoo can cause damage to the sweat glands. What about SMP?
SMP – damage to sweat glands?
Aug 7, 2018
A regular tattoo is not the same as SMP.
The SMP needles, with proper technique, are going in very shallow depth that is above the sweat glands. If this is the case, then no damage to the sebaceous glands.
