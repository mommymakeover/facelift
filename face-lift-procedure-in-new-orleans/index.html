Face Lift Procedure In New Orleans

The technical term for a facelift in New Orleans is a rhytidectomy. It is a kind of cosmetic surgery that aims to remove wrinkles and give a person a much more youthful appearance. A facelift involve excess facial skin removal and may or may not involve tightening any of the underlying tissues of the skin. The procedure may also be done by re-draping skin on the neck or face of the patient.

The first ever facelift done was in the year 1901 by a man called Eugene Hollander. In the records of the American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery, the facelift procedure was the 7th most popular aesthetic surgery performed just after other cosmetic surgeries such as breast augmentation, liposuction, eyelid surgery, a tummy tuck, rhinoplasty and breast reduction.

A Mini-lift May Only Include The Cheek And Neck Areas

The procedure for a conventional facelift is done by making an incision in the ear’s front and extending it up to the hairline. The incision then curves around the ear’s bottom part as well as behind. It usually ends near the hairline on the neck’s back.

After the incision, skin is then separated from the deeper tissues via a scissor or scalpel over the neck and cheeks. The deeper tissue is then tightened via sutures. After which, the skin is re-draped and the excess skin that is to be removed depends on the judgment of the surgeon. Once this is done, skin incisions are stapled or sutured closed.

Facelifts are necessary helpful especially in the elimination of skin folds that are loose in the cheeks or in the neck. There are areas however which may not be very well corrected by this procedure. This includes the perioral moundsmarionette lines and the nasolabial folds. For such areas, the better procedure is liposculpture or Botox.

Cosmetic Surgery Facelift

In any surgery, especially a cosmetic surgery facelift, there are risks involved. Though cosmetic procedures help in making someone feel positive about themselves, complications could also rise if boons and banes are not thoroughly considered.

First things first, what is a cosmetic surgery facelift and what does the procedure entail? The fact of the matter is that a cosmetic surgery facelift is a procedure that is utilized in order to restore the neck and face of a patient and make it smoother, attractive and more contoured. When completed, a cosmetic surgery facelift has the possibility to even take off decades from one’s face.

Mini Facelift New Orleans

So how would you know if you are a possible candidate for a cosmetic surgery facelift? Take note that there are a lot of factors to consider. If there are deep folds or lines running from your nose’s corner towards your mouth, or if there are deep wrinkle lines found on your cheeks, if your cheekbones are sagging, if you have loose skin folds, if your jaw line is no longer well defined, then you are a good candidate for a cosmetic surgery facelift in New Orleans.

The common risk involved after going through a cosmetic surgery facelift is scarring. Unfortunately, this is inevitable as skin is cut and therefore skin that is cut will eventually scar.

Other risks include tenderness and swelling. It is best to take a vacation from work so recuperation would be complete and proper.

There also exists the possibility of fluid or blood to be retained under the skin. Scars from the facelift procedure may also crust or turn numb. There is also a risk, although low, of experiencing nerve damage and contracting an infection.
