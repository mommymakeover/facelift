Increasingly, when you look in the mirror, you experience dissatisfaction and disappointment: after all, you feel still so young, but wrinkles, faded skin and a haggard face suggest the opposite. Age changes your face in several aspects at once. The system that regulates the hydration of the skin becomes less active, so the ability of the skin to straighten the facial wrinkles is reduced and over time they become deep.

The production of elastin and collagen by skin cells is reduced, which worsens the tone of the skin and leads to its sagging. The tone of the facial muscles also becomes weaker every year, and this further aggravates the problems that have arisen: wrinkles become deeper, and the face “hangs”, forming folds and creases in the most prominent places: on the nose, in the nasolabial triangle, in the paraorbital and near-lipic zones.

Preparing for a facelift.

1. Consultation of a plastic surgeon.

This is a very important point, where the doctor examines the condition of the soft tissues of the face and its bone base. This is done in order to most accurately assess the possibility of achieving the desired result, as well as provide for possible risks.

With the same purpose, the plastic surgeon finds out your expectations of the upcoming facelift surgery, and explains to you what you can get without compromising your health, and what steps you should not take.

And also informs you about those nuances of your appearance that can prevent you from achieving an ideal result. For example, a chamfered chin will not allow the creation of a classic neck line. It is extremely important to have an objective view and form real expectations about the facelift surgery: this will avoid unnecessary disappointment afterwards.

Your task at the consultative stage is extremely frank.

A plastic surgeon should be aware of all health problems, the way of your life, habits, including harmful ones, in order to minimize the risk of complications and provide the result that best suits your request.

If you have had previous operations, and there has been a formation of keloid scars, or bad coagulation of the blood, be sure to tell the doctor about it.

If the consultation was successful and the decision was taken to carry out a facelift in Virginia Beach, the doctor will acquaint you with the conditions of the hospital where you will stay the first 24 hours after the intervention, and also will necessarily voice the cost of the plastic surgery.

What do you need to do before a facelift?

Dr Shlomo Widder Lower Face Lift

  • Quit smoking two weeks before the operation: this will reduce the risk of such a serious complication, as necrosis the edges of the wound, which is characteristic of smokers;
  • To exclude the use of alcohol, so as not to disrupt the effect of anesthesia;
  • Stop (or stop for a while) taking medications that worsen blood coagulability: these include aspirin, some hormonal and psychotropic drugs, all anticoagulants;
  • Consult a neurologist to identify possible functional features from the facial nerve to avoid unpleasant neurological side effects after a facelift;
  • If you wear a short haircut, it makes sense to grow a few hair to hide the scars during their healing;
  • In advance agree on who will help you in the first days after discharge, since certain restrictions will be imposed on motor activity.

Preparation.

In the day appointed for the operation you come to the clinic and you are prepared for the intervention: the plastic surgeon applies the markings on which the incisions will be made, the assistant or nurse cuts the hair on the operating field, and the rest fixes the elastic bands in small tufts. Then you are transferred to the operating room.

Anesthesia.

In all cases of facelift, only general anesthesia is used, including intravenous and intubation. The action of anesthesia begins already at the stage of intravenous administration of the drug.

The course of the operation.

The surgeon treats the face with an antiseptic and proceeds directly to the operation. The techniques vary, but in most cases a classical access option is used, in which the incision is made in the occipital and temporal region, as well as in front of the auricle. The next step is the separation of the skin.

Looking For A Mini Facelift In Virginia Beach

Further, the plastic surgeon tightens the skin or musculocutaneous flap back slightly upward, visually evaluates the result and only then excised the excess tissue and, if necessary – removes some fat or fat redistribution produces to give the face a more precise contours. The process is completed by the application of intradermal and external sutures, in which thin drainage tubes are left. The dressing is superimposed and pressing the entire head.

Depending on the chosen technique of facelift, the operation can last from two to eight hours.

After operation.

In the next 2-3 days after a facelift, you are in a hospital under the supervision of doctors and nurses. If there are no complications during this period, you are discharged home to an outpatient home.

When you look in the mirror after removing the bandage, your reflection is unlikely to like you: swelling, bruising, local hyperemia of the seams and a face that may seem strange to you – frozen and tense. Cast aside doubts! Everything will be in order, you just need to be patient and wait a few weeks, after which the traces of facelift can easily be hidden by make-up.

The final effect can be estimated only after 6-8 months. But such a long wait is worth it, believe me! The the facial contour will become clear, the wrinkles will smooth out, the glow will shine, and the cosmetic sutures will be almost invisible.

Important!

The operation will restore your face to youth, but will not turn back time. The effect of facelift can last up to 10 years, and its duration depends on several factors: internal (genetic, hormonal) and external (lifestyle, nutrition, physical activity, careful care). The operation can be repeated in full or in part after a certain time, but this issue is decided in each case individually.
