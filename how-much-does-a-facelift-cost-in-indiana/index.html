How Much Does A Facelift Cost In Indiana?

The aging process of the face is of a natural nature and occurs gradually. Over the years, the skin and deeper tissues sag, wrinkles and pigmentation appear on the face. The task of facelift is to re-create the contours of the face, tighten the skin and underlying tissues, eliminate skin folds and remove excess fatty tissue (mainly in the chin area). Full facelift in Indiana includes a tightening of the cheeks, forehead and neck (these procedures can be performed in a complex or separately). If necessary, blepharoplasty can also performed (eyelid correction).

Facelift does not improve the general condition of the skin and does not remove fine wrinkles and pigmentation spots. A peeling procedure is required to correct these skin defects, which is recommended to be performed several months before the operation or several months after it.

Dr Greg Chernoff S Lift Surgery

Age is not a contraindication for a facelift. However, it should be remembered that as the skin ages, the skin loses its elasticity and its general condition worsens.

The surgeon will perform several photos of your face in different projections to fix the upcoming changes.

Preparation for surgery

  • You will need to do a blood test and, if necessary, undergo additional tests (ECG, chest X-ray, etc.).
  • For two weeks before the operation you need to stop taking aspirin and refrain from alcohol.
  • It is recommended to stock up with painkillers in advance, for example, opalgin and acamol. Do not use aspirin as an analgesic.
  • If you dye your hair, do this procedure no later than a week before the forthcoming operation.
  • 6 hours before the operation is not allowed to eat and drink. You can use chewing gum.
  • You should wear comfortable clothes and shoes on the day of surgery (preferably a blouse or shirt with a fastener in front). Some patients prefer to bring a wide-brimmed hat or a handkerchief for discharge from the clinic.

    • Dr. Mark Hamilton Lift Face Skin

  • You will meet with an anesthesiologist for examination and consultation. You will also need to sign an informed consent form for anesthesia.

Immediately before the operation, you will once again meet with a surgeon, who will mark on your face the places of the subsequent incisions.

Progress of the operation

An anesthesiologist will install an intravenous catheter through which drugs for anesthesia will be injected. As a rule, the operation is performed under local anesthesia, but using strong soothing agents, so that you will not experience pain.

During the operation, incisions are made along the border of hair growth, behind the auricles on the scalp.

Sometimes additional incisions are made on the forehead and under the chin. After the operation, a soft bandage will be applied on the face and head. Behind, from under the bandage, drainage tubes will drain away. At the end of the procedure, you will be transferred to a postoperative ward, where the medical staff will monitor your blood pressure, pulse and body temperature.

Facelift Results With Dr. Catherine Winslow

Postoperative period

After awakening, you will feel tension and pressing pain in the face and neck area. Nurses in the ward will give you an extra dose of painkillers. When your mind recovers completely, you can start drinking a little and get out of bed with the help of a nurse. As a rule, you are discharged the next day after the operation. Then drainages are removed, the bandage is removed. A nurse will help you wash your hair. Places of postoperative scars will be pasted with plasters.

Recovery period. How to behave at home?

After discharge from the hospital, you will be scheduled to meet with a doctor (usually one week after the operation).

  • It is recommended to wash your hair every day. In a day after the operation, swelling and bruising will appear on your face. These symptoms gradually take place within two weeks. Certain areas on the face may lose sensitivity. It is restored within a few months.

    • Mini Facelift In Indiana 6 Months After

  • In the first days after the operation, it is recommended to sleep and lie with a raised head.
  • In a few days after the operation, you can leave the house, applying makeup. However, sharp movements, physical activity and driving are not recommended.
  • The first time we recommend the use of analgesics every six hours.
  • Do not take aspirin or non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAID) – these drugs can cause bleeding.
  • In the first two weeks after the operation, do not dry hair with a hairdryer.
  • Two weeks after the operation, you will need to come for a second examination.
  • Contact your doctor or consult an emergency room if you experience any of the following symptoms: fever above 38 C, significant redness of the face, shortness of breath, severe swelling, or pain on one side.
