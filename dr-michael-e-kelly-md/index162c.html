Why swelling after the facelifting surgery? 4 days post-op.
swelling
Jan 19, 2018
Swelling is normal after surgery. It is the body's response to heal the injury caused by surgery. For most patients, the peak of swelling is at 48 hours, but this varies from patient to patient. I suspect that what you are experiencing is completely normal and will gradually resolve. It is hard to go through surgery because you want to look better, but initially look worse. Don't worry, it will ultimately be worth it.All the best

Can a facelift be done by a bicoronal incision? What are the risks? Hair loss? Scarring?
bicoronal incision
Jan 19, 2018
In my opinion, the bicoronal incision is far too aggressive to be used on the average facelift patient. The drawbacks of hair loss, pain and itching and numbness  make this suitable only for craniofacial and reconstructive surgery. In almost every instance, cosmetic goals can be achieved without the risks listed above through much smaller incisions, and less invasive approaches.I would talk to multiple surgeons before you go down the bicoronal road

Jan 19, 2018
In my experience, the facelift type is less important than the surgeon's skill in addressing these concerns. I find that liposuction of the jowls and fat grafting to the marionette lines is more important than the facelift type in smoothing the jawline . Ask to see before/after pictures from a board certified surgeon to give your self the best chance of achieving your facial goals.all the best
Upper lip paralysed after facelift and necklift 2 weeks ago. Is this permanent?
crooked smile
Jan 19, 2018
I'm sorry you have had this complication. It is very rare, but the good news is that in most instances, this will improve markedly with time. Often it returns to completely normal. The reason for that is there is cross innervation with other branches of the facial nerve and these will usually pick up the duties of the injured branch. Best of luck as you await the improvement.
