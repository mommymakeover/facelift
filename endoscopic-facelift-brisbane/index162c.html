Endoscopic Facelift Brisbane

Endoscopic facelift in Brisbane is becoming increasingly in demand for rejuvenating surgery among women and men over 45 years of age. Safety, non-traumatism, quick recovery and excellent results for many years are the main advantages of this modern technique, which earned well-deserved popularity. It is important to note that with endoscopic facelift, the effect on nerve endings, blood vessels and hair bulbs is minimal. Therefore, endoscopic lift is considered one of the most gentle methods for facelift.

About the method:

The most common area of application of endoscopic facelift is the upper third of the face, correction the shape and position of the eyebrows, changing the hairline, and correcting deep wrinkles (creases) in the nose and forehead areas.

Endocopic Face Lift Images

The operation is performed through miniature punctures, the size of which is not more than 15 mm. The use of advanced endoscopic equipment allows not only to reduce the time of the operation, but also to accelerate the healing processes.

Scars become invisible after 2-4 months, thanks to the use of laser and radio wave equipment, which contributes to the early regeneration of tissues. With regard to the technique of carrying out, with a pronounced sagging of the facial skin, after carrying out a tightening of the tissues, remove 1-2 cm of excess skin. Thus, it is possible to achieve a pronounced effect of face rejuvenation for 10-15 years and save the result for more than 10 years.

With age, the appearance of wrinkles and loss of elasticity is characteristic for the skin. Due to the development of modern cosmetology technologies, a new method of facial rejuvenation – endoscopic facelift can solve such problems. The indications predisposing to the use of this procedure are such age-related skin changes as: vertical wrinkles along the line of cheeks and between the eyebrows, horizontal forehead wrinkles, temporal wrinkles, “overhanging” of the skin, etc.

In addition, endoscopic facelift helps to hide traces of surgical operations in places inaccessible to someone else’s eyes: in the mouth, behind the ears, in the scalp, along the eyebrow line. In addition to the rejuvenating effect, this technique is able to harmoniously change the face: return the skin elasticity and make corrections to the shortcomings of the shape and volume of the face.

The operation of endoscopic facelift is divided into two procedures: lift the middle and upper parts of the face. An operation is performed under local anesthesia, and in cases of necessity of surgical intervention under general anesthesia.

Upper Endoscopic Facelift Photo

At the beginning of the procedure, several small incisions of up to 2 centimeters are made in the scalp. The endoscope with a small camera is inserted through the incisions, which reproduces the image on the monitor.

The use of an endoscope greatly facilitates the operation and shortens its duration. With the help of surgical mini-braces and mini-screws, the surgeon fixes soft tissues in the required position.

The exfoliation of the tissues from the bone is carried out in those layers that contain a small part of the blood vessels in order to avoid bleeding. Also, resections are not exposed to excess mobilized tissue, which are distributed under the skin.

This helps to avoid damage to capillaries, hair bulbs and nerve endings. During the operation of endoscopic facelift, individual correction and liposuction procedures are performed to achieve a better effect.

The main advantages of this operation are the absence of traces of surgery, absolute safety of the operation and the guarantee of a noticeable facial rejuvenation. Before deciding on endoscopic facelift, it is necessary to consult a specialist, since this procedure does not suit everyone.

The period of postoperative recovery is from 7 to 10 days, which also has a positive tendency for this procedure.
