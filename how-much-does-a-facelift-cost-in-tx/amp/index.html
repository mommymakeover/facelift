How Much Does A Facelift Cost In Texas?

A facelift (or rhytidectomy) in Texas is plastic surgery to remove wrinkles, sagging skin, correction of nasolabial folds and other signs of skin aging using tightening skin and underlying muscle redistribution face and neck skin excision with its excess.

Age-related changes in the facial soft tissues are part of the natural aging processes of the body.

They are caused by a decrease in the number of supporting collagen fibers in soft tissues and exposure to the skin of gravity.

With age, the tissues, eyebrows, upper and lower eyelids, cheeks are sagging downward. Suborbital and nasolabial furrows are formed and deepened.

The skin of the neck becomes flabby, horizontal wrinkles form, the cervical-chin angle disappears.

Various somatic diseases, exposure to harmful environmental factors and bad habits can accelerate the aging process of the facial soft tissues.

Often, professional cosmetology care is not enough by the age of 40 to preserve the natural beauty and youth of the face.

In what cases is rhytidectomy indicated?

The operation can be performed, if there is:

  • Ptosis of soft tissues cheeks below the edge of the lower jaw (so-called “jawls”);
  • Deep nasolabial folds;
  • Omitted the outer corners of the eyes;
  • Sagging skin of the forehead and eyebrows;
  • Ptosis of the eyelids;
  • Deep vertical wrinkles on cheeks;
  • Formation of wrinkles in the region of the nose, in the temporal region;
  • “Double chin;
  • Formation of deep wrinkles and folds around the neck.

Contraindications: when you can not do the operation?

Rhytidectomy in Texas is contraindicated in:

  • Oncological diseases;
  • Heavy diseases of internal organs;
  • Infectious diseases;
  • Violations of blood clotting.

What should I do before the operation?

Before the intervention, a general examination of the patient is performed, as before any surgical intervention. A detailed examination of the patient’s face is also conducted, goals, expectations from the operation and real possibilities are discussed. For 2 weeks before the operation, you should completely stop smoking, hormones, drugs that affect blood clotting (for example, aspirin, cough suppressants, etc.), a day before surgery it is recommended to perform a “hunger day” (only light food in small quantities). On the day of surgery, it is recommended not to eat or drink.

Anesthesia and surgery: doctor, I’m very afraid of pain …

Rhytidectomy is a long operation (4-5 hours), requiring general anesthesia (inhalation anesthesia). Lifting of individual areas of the face can be performed under local anesthesia or intravenous anesthesia. Operative access is chosen individually, but, in general, is carried out from the temporal region of the head, continuing along the anterior border of the auricle. The surgeon makes excision of excess skin tissue, facelift of the facial musculo-facial system (SMAS) and skin redistribution.

Postoperative period

The patient is in the hospital for 2-3 days after the operation under the supervision of doctors (in the case of any complicating diseases, such as diabetes, etc., the length of stay increases).

First 3-5 days, the doctor prescribes antibiotics and pain medications. Usually, the first dressing is performed the next day after the operation. In the future, dressings are performed on an outpatient basis.

Dr Benjamin Bassichis Facial Surgery Candidate Photo Results

Sutures are removed after about 1 week. Postoperative edema decreases in 10-20 days, however, facial massage, hair coloring, alcohol intake, smoking, sauna and exercise are contraindicated for at least 1-1.5 months.

You can wash your head and use cosmetics in a week. In most cases, patients return to work 2.5 to 3 weeks after surgery. If necessary, physiotherapy is prescribed, after 3-4 weeks it is advisable to conduct a course of lymphatic drainage massage.

The most common complications are:

  • Temporary hair loss around the cuts of the skin in the scalp, in the future their growth is restored independently;
  • Swelling of the face and bruises – using special creams disappear independently;
  • Skin pigmentation occurs in patients with sensitive and delicate skin with intradermal hemorrhages, disappears independently within six months after surgery.

Results after facelift surgery.

The final result is estimated after 6-12 months. It largely depends on the age of the patient, the initial condition of the skin, the presence of concomitant diseases and bad habits, the characteristics of the lifestyle (work, nutrition, etc.).

The same factors will determine the duration of the rejuvenating effect after the operation. Repeated facelifts can be performed at intervals of 5-10 years. Therapeutic cosmetic procedures significantly delay the re-operation period.

Mesotherapy, photorejuvenation, lymph drainage massage and masks fix the result for many years and are the prevention of the re-occurrence of soft facial ptosis and deep wrinkles.
