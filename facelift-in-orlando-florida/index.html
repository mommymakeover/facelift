Facelift In Orlando Florida

Is there a real way to stop the aging of the facial skin? The answer to this question lies in one short word – facelift.

Here we will tell you, what this concept means, and also answer all questions related to it.

Basic concepts

Facelift in Orlando is a plastic operation that allows to restore the elasticity of the skin, eliminating the sagging of the skin, smoothing wrinkles and getting rid of other visualized signs of aging.

During the procedure, the doctor removes the excess fat under the skin and tightens the facial muscles and redistributes the skin on the face and neck, cutting off at the same time excess skin.

A bit of history

The very first surgical operations for facelift were conducted in Europe in the early 20th century. However, this fact was advertised reluctantly, because in those years any change in appearance was considered a vanity and deeply condemned.

Minimal subcutaneous mobilization, excision of excess skin were applied in 1901 on a Polish aristocrat woman by a doctor Hollander. Further about similar operations were informed by many other experts, however, having been late for 30 years. In addition, leeches were used as a facelift in cosmetology. Subsequently, surgical facelifting has gained wide popularity.

When Do I Need a Facelift?

Cosmetic facelift can be indicated to patients who have the following symptoms:

  1. sagging of the eyelids (ptosis of soft tissues);
  2. Omitted of the outer corners of the eyes;
  3. The sagging of the skin of the forehead, brow and neck;
  4. Deep wrinkled furrows in the nose region and temporomandibular lobe of the face;
  5. Pronounced wrinkles, as well as wrinkles on the forehead and neck;
  6. Deep wrinkles in the nasolabial triangle;
  7. Strong ptosis of cheeks (“jowls”);
  8. Pronounced vertical wrinkles of the cheeks;
  9. Double chin.

If one or two of the above phenomena are detected, a mini facelift is possible. In other cases, it is desirable to conduct the surgery.

Since, face lift is an operation, the result of which depends not only on the knowledge and experience of surgeons, but also on the patient himself, there are the following recommendations that should be adhered to:

  • The patient’s age must be at least 40 and not more than 60 years;
  • Before a surgical intervention, a medical examination is necessary to determine the general health of the patient;
  • The patient’s skin should be of medium or satisfactory condition.

Contraindications to facelift

Like most surgical interventions, facelift in Orlando has some contraindications. Among them:

  • Severe diseases;
  • Eruptions of pustules, acne;
  • Infectious diseases;
  • Allergic reactions;
  • Oncological processes;
  • Violation of the process of blood coagulability;
  • Radiation therapy of the face in the anamnesis;
  • Excess weight of patients in the process of losing weight;
  • The possibility of an effective facelift without surgery.

Preparation for the procedure

When preparing for facelift, a medical examination is mandatory, designed to identify health problems. If there is no contraindications to the operation, the patient is assigned the operation date. It is necessary to refuse smoking and use of all hormonal preparations, as well as from medicines that can affect the process of blood coagulation two weeks before the operation. In addition, it is required to refrain from antitussive drugs. On the eve of surgery, you should eat light food. Immediately on the day of the operation, any water and food intake is prohibited.

The course of the procedure

The operation is performed by the patient under general anesthesia. Hair at the time of the procedure is pulled together into small bundles. The surgeon makes the incision from the area of one ear to the other, drawing a line along the hair growth, which makes the scars visually invisible. Then, the skin in the forehead area is stretched, its excess is excised. This tightens the facial muscles and eliminates wrinkles. After that, the braces are superimposed on the joint line, and the wound is closed with a bandage.

When to wait for the result?

Postoperative swelling of the skin completely disappears from the face 2 months after facelift, and the final result of the fully formed face can be observed in six months to a year. The final result will depend on many factors:

  • Initial condition and degree of skin elasticity;
  • Age of the patient;
  • Diseases;
  • Peculiarities of a way of life, etc.

The duration the effect of the procedure will depend on these factors. Usually it is from 5 to 10 years. Then you can perform and re-operation. Tightening of the soft tissues of the face sometimes can not do without some complications.

Among the most common are:

  • Bruising and swelling of tissues;
  • Slow healing and recovery;
  • Temporary decrease in tissue sensitivity;
  • Disturbance of skin pigmentation in patients with sensitive derma;
  • Temporary hair loss in the region of the incisions;
  • Damage to nerve endings associated with facial muscles.
