Endoscopic Facelift Houston

An endoscopic facelift in Houston is an advanced operative technology of face rejuvenation, as well as correction of age-related changes.

This surgical operation effectively allows you to perform facelift without prolonged rehabilitation and visible scars.

This procedure is recommended for people of middle age (from 35 to 50 years) with minor signs of aging of the facial skin.

Endoscopic facelift: the advantages.

A real breakthrough in plastic surgery was due to the appearance and use of the endoscope, as well as innovative tools in modern plastic surgery – the ability to perform an endoscopic facelift. This technique has many advantages.

Firstly, it is the absence of visible traces of surgical intervention. Scars after surgery are in places that are not visible to other people (among the hair on the head, in the oral cavity). Manipulations are performed with punctures in the forehead area, as well as from the oral mucosa. In the case of a neck lift, only one small incision is made in the region of the chin.

Secondly, significant rejuvenation is achieved thanks to the latest technology – there is a deep vertical reposition of tissues without tension, which other methods do not.

In contrast to the traditional facelift, an endoscopic facelift besides the skin, facial muscles and fat cells, also move the vessels and nerves, and therefore the rejuvenating effect becomes pronounced.

Endoscopic facelift in Houston allows you to perform a noticeable facial rejuvenation, as well as make it more harmonious, giving it the missing volume.

Third, an endoscopic facelift reduces the risk of hair loss, which often occurs as a result of a standard facelift. When using the endoscopic method, the area of the skin on which there is hair, is not removed, so there are no prerequisites for future hair loss.

Fourthly, this surgical technique significantly reduces the rehabilitation period, and significantly reduces the number of complications. This is achieved due to non-traumatic and minimally invasive operations.

Endoscopic facelift: indications.

The skin begins to lose elasticity and elasticity at the age of 35-50 years, tissues on the face go down, wrinkles and ptosis are observed, as well as. All this makes the facial contours not so tight and clear as in the young years, and the appearance is not so attractive. Endoscopic facelift can be recommended during this period.

This operation will eliminate:

  • Constant gloomy and weary facial expression;
  • Transverse and longitudinal wrinkles on the nose and forehead;
  • Strongly “overhanging” eyebrows;
  • Sagging tissues in the cheekbones and cheeks;
  • Lowered corners of the mouth;
  • Presence of nasolabial folds.

Endoscopic facelift in Houston helps to get rid of age-related changes, as well as from individual characteristics of soft tissues that cause negative emotions on the face – anger, gloominess, fatigue, resentment, etc. However, this kind of facelift is not indicated to everyone. The decision on the possibility and expediency of the procedure is made by the surgeon during the consultation.

Endoscopic facelift: contraindications

Contraindications to the endoscopic facelift are standard, as for any other surgical operation:

  • oncological diseases;
  • Acute, inflammatory, infectious diseases of the body;
  • Severe diabetes;
  • bleeding disorder;
  • Age is more than 50 years, at which deep layers of the skin lose their elasticity.

Endoscope lift of the upper face area

Endoscopic facelift of the upper third of the face is performed in a hospital under general or local anesthesia and lasts up to 1.5-2 hours. The surgeon makes 2-6 incisions length of 1.5-2 cm on the scalp. An endoscope is inserted under the skin through them, sending an image to the monitor screen, as well as the instruments by which the surgeon exfoliates the soft tissues from the bone, tightens it and fixes its in a new position. The risk of bleeding is minimal.

Often, the surgeon does not resected the excess of the mobilized tissue, but only redistributes it. Endoscopic lifting of eyebrows and forehead skin does not injure nerve endings, vessels and hair follicles, which is typical of the standard technique. In addition, the use of endoscopic techniques can shorten the duration of the operation. Endoscopic lifting helps to tighten the skin of the forehead, eliminate wrinkles, model attractive eyebrows, make a more expressive look, and also eliminate “crow’s feet” near the eyes. It may not be necessary to perform the upper blepharoplasty.

Endoscopic lifting of the upper part of the face can reduce the activity of facial muscles between the eyebrows, raise eyebrows, reduce the activity of the forehead muscle, smooth out wrinkles in the corners of the eyes. The recovery period after surgery is from one to two weeks. You need to wear a special compression bandage for five days after surgery.

Endoscopic lifting the middle and lower facial area.

Endoscopic lifting of the middle zone of the face helps to restore the volume characteristic for a young face, smooth the nasolabial folds, and also raise the middle third of the face.

The specialist makes two incisions of 1.5-2 cm in length in the hairy area of the incision zone, as well as two incisions in the oral cavity under the upper lip.

The soft tissues are separated from the periosteum, then stretched and fixed in a new position, excess tissue and skin are excised. Endoscopic lift of the middle zone of the face is performed in a hospital under anesthesia and lasts up to 3 hours. The recovery period after the operation is from 7 to 12 days.

Endoscopic lifting of the lower and upper parts of the face can be performed simultaneously, sequentially or separately. This operation allows to achieve a noticeable uplift of soft tissues with the formation of a clear contour of the face, effectively removes nasolabial folds, raises the corners of the mouth, zygomatic tissues, partially lifts the skin of the face in the cheek area.

Endoscopic lift of the neck is performed with a small incision in the chin area. The operation allows to make the transition from the chin to the neck clear and maximally pronounced due to the movement of tissues.

Combination of endoscopic facelift with other procedures.

An endoscopic facelift is well combined with other cosmetic procedures – for example, blepharoplasty, liposuction and a lower facelift, neck lift, lipofilling, etc. Moreover, often such combined operations give the best effect of skin rejuvenation. The sequence and number of additional procedures can be properly established only by a qualified plastic surgeon.
