Effectiveness Of Thermage Skin Treatments

Is Thermage affordable and can you benefit from it?

Aging can have detrimental effects on the appearance of the skin. The presence of fine lines and loss of elasticity frequently increases with time.

Established cosmetic options like laser treatments, facelifts or retinoid creams are effective but may leave unattractive scars.

In recent years, a quick, noninvasive therapeutic approach called thermage has been devised. Thermage is an innovative dermatological procedure that treats wrinkles and sun damage by utilizing radiofrequency-generated energy to firm and smooth skin.

Results are astounding, and thermage cost is relatively low when compared to its cosmetic benefit.

Thermage treatments are usually between $1,000 to $5,000. Nevertheless, how does it work?

Thermage uses a dual-featured probe, which combines the production of powerful radiofrequencies with a cooling function to protect the epidermis. The procedure heats collagen within the skin for instantaneous firming, while simultaneously stimulating additional collagen production for more pronounced results over time.

Is Thermage Effective For Face

During the treatment, which may require up to two hours, a thermage probe transmits heat deep into the skin’s dermal layers while releasing a cool spray to protect the skin’s surface. Heat-generated tissue contractions in the deep layers of skin reduce epidermal lines and wrinkles, and the triggering of new collagen formation further enhances the firmness of the patient’s skin from deep within. The physician determines the level of heat used in the procedure and gauges the amount based on patient comfort and maximized results.

Although no surface damage is sustained, treatments are painful, and anesthesia, such as anesthetizing creams or intravenous drugs, is required. Pain medicines are typically indicated before the treatment to aid in minimizing discomfort. Other skin tightening procedures, such as dermal abrasion and laser-induced resurfacing, traumatize the skin’s surface and may cause scarring.

Thermage patients enjoy the benefits without the scarring. While general discomfort still ensues, thermage has progressed since its introduction and now produces better outcomes with less pain.

The lack of external trauma helps lower thermage cost and procedural risk. Side effects are extremely rare and usually resolve in a couple of weeks; they include swelling, blistering and reddening at the application site. Unlike facelift patients who have a recovery period of several weeks, thermage patients are often advised to resume work immediately.

Improvement to the texture and appearance of skin is generally noticeable immediately following treatment. Over the next several months, the skin becomes firmer and increasingly youthful. Treatment results remain intact for up to 24 months. Frequently, desired results are attained from the first treatment, but multiple treatments are sometimes beneficial. The physician advises the patient of what may be right for her.

Patient Expectations With Thermage

Though Thermage is a non-surgical treatment to tighten and rejuvenate skin, the patient still should know what to expect during and after the treatment. The procedure, it must be said, is expensive. Thermage cost for face are usually between $1,000 to $5,000. The patient should discuss these fees with their doctor before the treatment begins.

Thermage Can Be Used At The Face, Neck, Decolletage, Arms, Legs And More

Unlike surgery, the Thermage treatment doesn’t require the patient to do anything out of the ordinary, like fast overnight. Blood and urine tests aren’t required, though it’s not indicated for pregnant women or if there’s a malignant lesion or suspicion of a malignant lesion in the area that’s going to be treated. People who wear pacemakers or similar implanted devices should also not have the treatment.

Generally, the patient wont need anesthesia. During the treatment, the patient feels a coolness on the area of the skin thats being treated, then a heating or pinching sensation that’s a sign that the treatment is working to break down the collagen in the lower levels of skin. This is what leads to the lifting and tightening of the skin. The patient may then feel another sensation of coolness. Another benefit of no anesthesia is that the patient can remain awake and let the doctor know what they’re feeling. This helps the doctor adjust the treatment to make the patient more comfortable and also lets them know that the treatment is working.

Thermage Do Not Require Any Incisions, Tissue Removal Or Special Aftercare

The facial treatment can last from about 45 minutes to an hour, depending on how much of the skin is being treated. Thermage can also be used on the abdomen, the buttocks, the legs and other places where the skin sags or bulges.

The patient who undergoes Thermage treatment can usually return to their usual routine right after the surgery, though they might want to rest a bit if it’s possible. Some patients might experience swelling or redness immediately after the treatment, but this will fade over a short time, usually within a day. They should continue with their usual skin regiment, or follow a regiment the doctor recommends. It’s important that they avoid too much exposure to the sun and use sunscreen.

The patient should see improvements in the look and feel of their skin right away. The improvements continue over a period of about two to six months and can last for years, depending on what type of skin the patient has, their exposure to sun, how their skin ages and other factors. While Thermage can be pricey, many patients are very satisfied with their outcome.

This gives the skin a completely new and fresh look. The entire procedure usually takes one hour during which you might experience some little discomfort which clears immediately after the procedure. The skin gets a pink coloration which gradually fades after a few hours. One single treatment is enough to see the change in your face. The good thing about this procedure is that you will not require an aesthetic afterwards and it can be done on all skin types successfully.

Ultherapy has been unavailable for quite some time hence thermage has become the better option.
