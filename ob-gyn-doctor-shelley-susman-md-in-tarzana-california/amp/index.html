When is it okay to shave or wax again after labiaplasty?
Shaving and Waxing after Labiaplasty
Apr 19, 2018
I would certainly let the lasered edges of the incision heal before waxing. Shaving around the area of reduction would be ok, but the reduced labia should heal first. I see my patients back in the office weekly to check on their post op progress and would like to see about 3-4 weeks go by before waxing is done on the labia., if at all. Alternately, you could consider laser hair removal in the area after the labia has healed.
Does insurance cover laser rejuvenation for urinary incontinence?
Insurance coverage for urinary incontinence and laser vaginal procedure
Apr 19, 2018
Insurance will not cover laser rejuvenation for urinary incontinence. Many offices have payment plans, and CareCredit to help with costs. You can be signed up right away in the office. I would recommend that you have an exam by the gynecologist first to determine the cause of your incontinence so a proper treatment plan can be devised.
At 72, I have a mild prolapsed bladder and in need of vaginal tightening. How is it repaired?
Prolapsed Bladder and Vaginal Rejuvenation
Apr 19, 2018
Surgically a vaginal “anterior repair” will correct a bladder which has herniated into the vaginal area. This is a procedure done through the vagina, as an outpatient. This procedure is generally not done to tighten the vaginal vault. Laser vaginal rejuvenation will help strengthen the vaginal tissue, but is not considered a corrective procedure for a prolapsed bladder. I recommend an exam by the Gynecologist to determine your exact issues and formulate a plan for treatment.
