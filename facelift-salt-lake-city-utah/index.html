SMAS Facelift

This is the most effective surgical facelift in Salt Lake City. The optimal age for such operations is from 40 to 50 years, but it is possible to conduct SMAS-operations in a deeper age (up to 65 years).

There are classic (surgical), endoscopic and ultrasound SMAS facelift. The best results (visually rejuvenating for 10-15 years) are given by procedures with direct surgical intervention: the effect of this operation lasts for 10-15 years, but in the future the patient looks significantly younger than the real age.

It is enough to compare the photos before and after a facelift – the result can be seen at a glance.

How is the classical operation performed?

  • The surgeon makes an incision that begins in the temporal region of the growth line, bends around the ear and ends behind the ear.
  • Skin flaps with aponeurosis are stretched, separated from the underlying fascia.

  • Liposuction is performed if there is excess fatty deposits.
  • The complex is stretched (the skin is smoothed out) and is attached to the temporal fascia (in the places of incisions).
  • After fixing SMAS, the skin tissue without tension is attached to its place, and excess skin is removed.
  • Sutures and sterile bandages.
  • In some cases, drainage is installed in the wounds.
  • The whole operation takes from one and a half to three hours. The next few days need to be in the hospital, followed by a recovery period of up to three months.

Subcutaneous facelift

The subcutaneous facelift is less traumatic than a deep SMAS facelift – the risk of complications is much lower and the restoration of the skin is faster. However, the operation is not as effective in the presence of deep folds and gives a less durable result.

What are the best candidates for subcutaneous facelift?

In this case, the plastic surgery removes only the external aging factors, since the muscle and adipose tissue are not affected during the operation.

Advantages

  • Removes external age manifestations
  • Significantly lower risk of complications
  • Faster recovery period
  • The facial nerve is not affected.

Disadvantages

  • Does not correct deep defects.
  • Validity is lower than that of SMAS facelift.

How is the endoscopic SMAS facelift performed?

  • Punctures are usually 4-5 incisions less than 1 cm in the scalp.
  • Introduction to the puncture of the endoscope with a video camera, the translation of the image on the screen.
  • Separation of dermal tissue and its structure, fixing it in a new position.
  • Then the stitches are applied.

During the operation, surgeon can form a new shape of the eyebrows, cut or weaken the muscles that form a crease between the eyebrows or wrinkles on the forehead, and also emphasize cheekbones or raise the corners of the eyes – the decorative effect persists for many years.

Endoscopic facelift using endotin

Endotines are special implants that are implanted under the skin and soften the age-related manifestations. They create an artificial frame, to which the facial tissues are attached. This method is excellent for patients who are morally not ready for surgical correction and the plastic surgery. Facelift by endotins refers to minimally invasive methods – special threads and fixatives are introduced through small punctures in undistinguished places (near or behind the auricle).

Fixing tapes and plates of different shapes are selected individually – depending on the shape of the face and the installation site.

They are made from a gradually absorbable biopolymer and are equipped with small denticles, which on one side are attached to the bone tissue, and on the other – to the inner subcutaneous layer.

Threads and retainers gently distribute the tension of the facial muscles and skin.

After a certain period, endotins dissolve, and instead they form a similar skeleton of connective tissue, which performs the same functions.

Thread Lift As an Alternative to Full Facelift

Salt Lake City Facelift with the help of special threads is an alternative to surgical methods of facelift. The method involves the introduction of special threads into the skin – they create a framework and support the tissues in the given position. The end result does not depend on how much the facelift is worth: it is important to choose the material, the number and location of the threads, and also to introduce them correctly, so that in the future they do not appear under the skin and do not bring discomfort.

There are several types of thread facelift:

  • Threads of gold. This method has already become a classic – reinforcement strengthens the natural circulation of blood and provokes the production of collagen, which makes the skin more fresh and elastic.
  • Degradable threads (caprolactone and L-lactic acid). Fix the tissues in the desired position with the help of miniature hooks.
    In the process of dissolution of threads in the skin, collagen is intensively produced, which replaces artificial reinforcement. The effect of the procedure is up to 3 years.
  • Propylene threads are equipped with special hooks, which allow to shift the tissues of the face and tighten the contours.
