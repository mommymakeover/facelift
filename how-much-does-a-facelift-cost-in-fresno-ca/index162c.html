1. What is best Age for First Facelift?

There is no unambiguous answer to this question. Age changes in each person proceed in an individual way, there is no specific age for the operation.

Depending on the degree to which these or those age-related changes are expressed in particular problem areas, in some cases it makes sense to intervene at the age of 45, and sometimes this procedure can be shown only in 60 years of age.

The task of the responsible doctor is to explain to the patient who consults him for advice, which procedures are appropriate from a medical point of view, and which of them can be implemented.

2. How Long Does a Facelift Last?

When the operation is performed correctly, the effect of facelift in Fresno is retained for 10 years.

3. How does facelift affect on the facial expression?

If the face lift is done correctly, the facial expression can not suffer. The fact is that when using the SMAS method, the surgeon does not stretch the skin, but the fascia underneath.

4. Can a facelift be repeated?

Experts advise not to resort to facelift more often than once every ten years. In total it is recommended to do no more than three operations.

5. What is the difference between mini facelift and traditional facelift?

Mini facelift in Fresno is called a very limited surgical intervention, from which you should not expect any staggering results. It is advisable to perform this operation at the age of up to forty-five years, in the absence of excess skin and the preservation of their elasticity. The advantages of the method include a short recovery period.

6. Which areas of the face can be corrected?

Facelift allows to correct the most different parts of it. So, forehead lift is used to correct the line of eyebrows, age wrinkles, superior sulcus eyelids and facial expressions. The lift of the middle part of the face makes it possible to correct nasolabial wrinkles, tear sacks, the area of cheekbones and circles under the eyes. Cheeks lift is necessary to eliminate skin folds in this area and to perform partial correction of nasolabial wrinkles. Double chin and other defects of the neck are also eliminated with a facelift.

7. What is the middle zone of the face?

The middle zone of the face covers the lower edge of the lower eyelids and extends up to the cheekbones.

8. Does the facelift help get rid of crow’s feet and wrinkles around the mouth?

Correction of crow’s feet can be performed during a forehead lift, but the best results will be provided by a lateral eyebrow lift. Wrinkles around the mouth can not be removed surgically: for this purpose, as a rule, laser resurfacing and peeling are used.

9. What results can be achieved with a forehead lift?

With the help of a forehead lift, corrugation on the forehead are eliminated and eyebrows are raised. With the help of the eyebrow lift it is possible to get rid of the sad expression of the face, as well as overhanging eyebrows.

10. Where are the seams after the forehead lift?

Depending on the method used, scars can be located in the scalp and are completely invisible (coronary forehead lift), or along the border of hair and forehead growth. After endoscopic facelift, small scars remain in the area of the hairline.

Fresno Face Lift Short Incision

11. What results can be achieved after the midface lift?

Midface lift is aimed at correction of nasolabial wrinkles, removal of lacrimal sacks and circles under the eyes and to restore volume in the area of cheekbones.

12. Where are the seams after the midface lift?

When performing an isolated midface lift, the sutures are most often located in the lower eyelid right under the line of eyelash growth. If the cheek lift is performed simultaneously, additional postoperative sutures can be applied.

13. What results can be achieved with the help cheek lift?

Cheeks lift makes it possible to correct chubby cheeks (“cheeks of a hamster”), to eliminate wrinkles in the cheek area and to get rid of nasolabial wrinkles to a moderate degree.

14. Where are the seams after cheek lift?

During classical cheek lift, the seams are from the hair growth line in the temporal region downward along the ear cartilage to the earlobe, from there along the inner side of the ear towards the hair growth line.

15. Will liposuction help get rid of the second chin?

Provided that, the skin has retained a sufficient degree of elasticity, liposuction is able to rid of the second chin, consisting mainly of adipose tissue.

16. Can be carried out simultaneously lift several zones of the face?

Typically performed simultaneously lift neck and cheek lift. Sometimes, the correction of the middle part of the face and the tightening of the cheeks are combined. Combined correction of the forehead and cheeks area is practically not applied.

17. What complications can occur after a facelift?

The most common complication after such plastic surgery is bleeding, as well as possible long-term tissue healing. In the event that a facelift has been performed incorrectly, there may be damage to the facial nerves. The rule is the occurrence of swelling and a violation of sensitivity, which occurs during the recovery period.

18. What is the duration of the hospital stay?

After the facelift in Fresno, CA, the patient is usually discharged from the hospital after one or two days.

19. Will there be pain after the operation?

Pain after the facelift surgery is almost not observed, but there may be a feeling of discomfort and tension of the skin, which will pass independently after the recovery period.

20. How long does the recovery period last?

After this, patients are recommended from two to four weeks of rest, then they can return to their usual lifestyle.
