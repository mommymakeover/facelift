Types of facelift

Depending on the required result, a face lift may differ in the method of its conduct. Accordingly, our clinic of plastic surgery offers different types of facelift:

Thread Lift

It is most common among women, which is explained by minimal surgical intervention and a short rehabilitation period after the procedure (usually no more than 3 days). This operation is performed under local anesthesia and lasts no more than an hour, and the effect can last up to 5 years.

Endoscopic facelift

Based on the name it can be understood that the procedure is carried out with the help of endoscopic equipment, due to which the risk of tissue damage is practically absent. One of the advantages of this operation can be called quick and easy recovery period (about 7 days), and a long-term result (up to 10 years).

This operation is classical. When it is carried out, excess skin is removed and tissues are tightened. It is performed under general anesthesia, and the recovery period after the procedure is approximately 2 weeks. However, even after the expiration of this period, the patient should refrain from heavy loads for about another month. The duration of the result is 8-12 years.

Full facelift

For those who want to achieve a more visible and long-lasting effect of facelift, our clinic offers an operation such as a full face and neck lift. Under this procedure is meant the removal of excess skin and tightening of soft tissues through surgical intervention.

Dr. George Commons Facial Reconstruction Surgery

Before the operation, specialists conduct a preliminary assessment of skin condition and structure, identify the severity of the defects. The procedure is performed under general anesthesia and lasts, as a rule, no more than 4 hours. The period of postoperative recovery is approximately 2 weeks, however, even after full recovery the patient should refrain from heavy loads and prolonged exposure to the sun for about a month.

This procedure can be contraindicated in the event that:

  • The patient has oncological diseases or infection.
  • The patient has poor blood clotting.
  • There is a pathology of the internal organs.
  • If the patient is suffering from diabetes mellitus.
  • Before agreeing to an operation, you should always consult with specialists of our clinic.
