Statement: Dr. Michael I. Echavez is a double-board-certified facial plastic surgeon practicing in San Francisco, California. Though he is most renowned for his expertise in performing cosmetic surgeries of the face and neck, he is also highly skilled at administering minimally- and noninvasive aesthetic treatments, including laser skin resurfacing, body contouring, Botox, and dermal fillers such as Juvéderm, Restylane, Sculptra Aesthetic, and Radiesse.

In addition to his warm, patient, and attentive bedside manner, Dr. Echavez is known for his perfectionism. His loyal clients travel from throughout the West Coast to be treated by him at his Bay Area practice. His meticulous, artistic eye and precise hands make Dr. Echavez exceptionally talented at providing subtle yet powerfully transformative results, enhancing the appearance and confidence of every patient he touches.

Dr. Echavez is a native of the Bay Area who graduated with his bachelor’s degree from the University of California, Berkeley. He went on to earn his medical degree from University of California, San Francisco, where he also completed his residencies in general surgery and otolaryngology-head and neck surgery. Dr. Echavez then temporarily relocated to Cincinnati, Ohio, where he completed a rigorous fellowship in facial plastic and reconstructive surgery before returning home to open private practices in San Francisco, California and Seattle, Washington.

Dr. Echavez is one of a select group of physicians who has been certified by not one but two medical boards: the American Board of Facial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery and the American Board of Otolaryngology-Head and Neck Surgery. This dual board certification, coupled with over 28 years of experience, means that Dr. Echavez has a comprehensive mastery of anatomy from the neck up, making him a highly sought-after provider of both aesthetic and reconstructive care to patients in the Bay Area.

Passionate about advancing cosmetic surgery techniques in the medical community, Dr. Echavez is on the clinical faculty at University of California, San Francisco, where he teaches future generations of physicians. He also frequently teaches other physicians around the country how to best administer facial contouring fillers as a trainer for several injectable companies.

Many of Dr. Echavez’s patients cite his talent at administering injectables. He was one of the very first plastic surgeons to treat patients with Sculptra in the United States and is very skilled at using this product, as well as other dermal fillers and injectables. Dr. Echavez not only performs facial surgeries and treatments, but also offers noninvasive fat reduction treatments, including CoolSculpting and SculpSure, and skin tightening treatments like Fractora and FaceTite.

Dr. Echavez stays active professionally as a frequent guest lecturer at conferences nationwide, sharing his injectable techniques. He is also a current member of the American Academy of Facial and Plastic Reconstructive Surgery.

Is Sculptra bad?
Sculptra
Nov 16, 2023
In my opinion, Sculptra is a great product when used correctly. I have been using it for over 22 years and have had very few problems with the results. Treatment with Sculptra normally involves more than one treatment session and results are gradual.
Are there safer alternatives to Sculptra for subtle volume in face?
Option for subtle volume in face
Nov 6, 2023
There are a variety of fillers that could be used to add volume to the face. The results could be subtle or more dramatic, depending on how much is used. I think Sculptra is a good option, with the main advantage being that the results last relatively long. I think you could also get a good result with Voluma. The placement and amount used would be able to give you a subtle result without making your face triangular.
Can I get Chin Filler with Chin Implant?
Filler after chin implant
Nov 3, 2023
Yes, it is possible to place a filler in the chin after placement of a chin implant. The filler is normally placed more superficially than where the implant would be. You should be sure to let the physician treating you know that you have a chin implant prior to a filler treatment.
I have a mole very close to my right eye. Can I get rid of it?
Mole near eye
Nov 1, 2023
The mole near your eye could be removed fairly easily in the office. I would recommend consulting with a facial plastic surgeon, plastic surgeon or oculoplastic surgeon.
30 year old female, 5 vials of Sculptra, what are my options with overproduction of collagen?
Options for overproduction of collagen after Sculptra
Oct 23, 2023
The simplest option to improve over-filling after Sculptra treatment is giving it more time. It will gradually go down by itself with more time. I do not think that phototherapy would be helpful to lessen the volume.
CoolSculpting for banana rolls?
CoolSculpting for banana rolls after silicone
Oct 22, 2023
CoolSculpting can be used to treat banana rolls, but the treatment will only help to reduce fat in the area. The procedure would not affect silicone in the treatment area. An examination of you would be needed to determine if you might be a good candidate for CoolSculpting or not.
How do I get rid of these nasolabial folds?
Treatment of nasolabial folds
Oct 17, 2023
I'm not sure if the photo you provided is before or after the filler that you had. Based on the photo, it does appear to me that there is some volume loss in the cheeks. Normally, adding volume in the cheeks would improve the nasolabial folds. The only other treatment I can think of that you haven't tried that potentially could help would be direct excision of the nasolabial folds. It's difficult to tell if you might be a good candidate for this procedure, just based on the photo.
Filler after chin surgery?
Filler 1 year after chin surgery
Oct 1, 2023
