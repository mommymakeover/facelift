Statement: Dr. Ronald Schuster has been a leading, board-certified plastic surgeon for over three decades. Based in Baltimore, Maryland, he is renowned for his expertise in a range of plastic surgery procedures, including rhinoplasty, facelifts, neck lifts, breast augmentation, breast lifts, liposuction, and tummy tucks. He also offers nonsurgical treatments such as laser resurfacing, dermal fillers, Botox, and CoolSculpting body contouring.

Dr. Schuster earned his medical degree from the University of Maryland School of Medicine in Baltimore. He went on to complete a five-year general surgery residency at Sinai Hospital in Baltimore and then served a three-year residency and fellowship program in plastic surgery at Washington University’s Barnes-Jewish Hospital in St. Louis, Missouri. He remained in St. Louis to complete a fellowship in craniofacial surgery at the Cleft Palate and Craniofacial Deformities Institute at the St. Louis Children’s Hospital before establishing his private practice in Baltimore.

Throughout his career, Dr. Schuster has dedicated time to researching and improving on advanced plastic surgery techniques. The American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery awarded him a Certificate of Excellence for his study of blood supply during facelift procedures, which helped identify safer facelift methods. Today, he shares his surgical knowledge as a clinical instructor in the Division of Plastic Surgery at the Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore.

Consistently selected as a top doctor by Baltimore magazine, Dr. Schuster uses the latest technology to produce natural-looking results for his patients. He prides himself on the quality of care his patients experience, from their very first consultation through recovery. His practice is built on a tradition of excellence, personalized care and exquisite, natural-looking results, with a signature experience centered on each patient’s unique physique, lifestyle and goals.

In addition to practicing privately, he serves as Chief of the Plastic Surgery Division at Northwest Hospital in Randallstown, Maryland.

Board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery, Dr. Schuster is an active member of the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, the American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery, the American Medical Association, and the American Society for Laser Medicine and Surgery.

Prominent nasal spine/nasolabial angle - what is achievable with rhinoplasty?
Anterior nasal spine
Mar 14, 2023
The fullness at the junction of the nose and upper lip can be caused by a few things. A prominent anterior nasal spine is possible but not as frequent as you might think. You should be able to feel the hard bone protruding. More frequently it is the caudal septum which is too long. Finally, the tip might be pulled too far forward causing. Tenting of the skin. All are easily fixed with rhinoplasty. This is where a good exam is really needed. Good luck.
