Facelift in OKC – INTEGRATED APPROACH

The complex approach includes interventions on both soft facial tissues (skin, subcutaneous fatty tissue, muscular-aponeurotic system), and on hard tissues (facial bones).

The objectives of surgery for facelift and the facial contour correction:

  • Elimination of ptosis of soft cheek tissues, displacement or removal of the fatty body of the cheek (fat pad);
  • Correction of the contour of the lower edge of the lower jaw (is carried out both by moving soft tissues, and by increasing the volume of the bone tissue of the lower jaw in areas most prone to bone resorption with age);
  • Formation of the expressed cervical-chin angle;
  • Reduction in the expression of nasolabial folds (movement of soft tissues, combination of liposuction of the area above the nasolabial fold and lipofilling of the fold);
  • Correction of the tissues of the cheek area (movement of soft tissues, lipofilling, injection of hydroxyapatite granules into the periosteum to increase the volume of the bone structure).

Thus, when performing surgical intervention of age-related changes in facial tissues, the main task of the surgeon is to maintain a natural and harmonious appearance after the operation, as well as in the selection of appropriate techniques for surgical intervention.

When planning facelift surgery in Oklahoma city, it is necessary to take into account the fact that all age changes on the face are mainly observed in the front part of the face, that is, in the areas where the facial muscles are directly related to the skin.

These muscles are involved in talking, expressing emotions. In a word, this department of the face is responsible for communication. Mimic muscles are located around the eyeballs, along the periphery of the oral cavity.

In contrast to this area, in the side sections of the face, changes associated with age have minimal manifestations. This is explained by the anatomical structure of the chewing area of the face, the place of attachment of the muscles and their function.

Liquid Facelift Okc

Muscles of this area belong to the group of chewing, respectively the place of their attachment, only bone structures. The only muscle that lies superficially and directly connected with the skin (subcutaneous muscle) passes in the lower 1/3 face and does not rise above the level of the corner of the mouth.

Thus, the face can be divided into two zones – the mobile front and the slow moving side. The border of these areas is a vertical line, going through the so-called restraining ligaments, which are located just along the border of the anterior and lateral areas of the face and connect the skin, subcutaneous structures and muscles with the bones of the facial skull.

These questions of the anatomy of the facial structures were studied in detail and published by Dr. Mendelson in 2001.

Mini Facelift Okc

The main part of the methods of tightening the soft tissues of the face is based on the intervention in the area of the side of the face with a slight transition to the front.

Based on the anatomy described above, and taking into account the presence of fixative ligaments along the border of the facial front region, the movement of the flap of the superficial musculo-aponeurotic system (SMAS) peeled off during the operation is limited by the tension of the ligaments with slight changes in the position of the tissues in the the front of the face.

Extending the flap forward further is accompanied by a large area of skin detachment and subcutaneous soft tissue, which leads to greater trauma, a risk of thinning the SMAS flap and, as a result, its insolvency.

In addition, this leads to the appearance of swelling with the need to drain the postoperative wound (evacuation of accumulating blood and wound fluid through silicone tubes).

The long-term result of facelift surgery in OKC depends on the accuracy of certain conditions on the part of the plastic surgeon. Mobilization of the musculo-aponeurotic layer should be performed taking into account the localization of fixative ligaments with maximum relaxation. Also, a minimal skin detachment should be performed to prevent post-operative accumulation of blood under the skin. Ideal when the operation is performed with minimal trauma and maximum effect.

Space Lift

An example of compliance with the conditions specified above, is a variant of facelift by the method of space lift. This technique was proposed by Dr. B. Mendelson.

Non Surgical Facelift Okc

The key points of this surgical intervention are based on the fact that between the facial muscles there are so-called fascial spaces filled with connective tissue. Spaces perform an isolating function, that is, as if to separate the muscles from each other and are located in the mobile areas of the face. Each space has clearly traced boundaries. The retaining ligaments are located at the boundaries of these spaces.

The surgeon, based on the topographic anatomy of the location of the spaces, must penetrate into the spaces and, by crossing the restraining ligaments, mobilize the SMAS flap.

With a clear hit inside the space, the probability of damage to the facial nerve is minimal, since all its fibers pass beyond this anatomical formation.

It should be noted, with this method of SMAS mobilization, the flap of the musculo-aponeurotic system is very well moved.

It is also ensured reliable fixation of the displaced flap due to the fact that the walls of the space are represented by a dense fascial tissue, which is well retained by means of superimposed seams.

Thus, the surgeon, based on the anatomical structure of the face, can perform the following transformations:

  • Move the tissue of the neck area,
  • Make the cervical angle more acute and pronounced,
  • Eliminate sagging on the lower edge of the lower jaw,
  • Move complex of tissues of the middle zone of the face and temples.

You should remember about such important points as the inborn positions of some facial structures. For example, the congenital low position of the hyoid bone and the muscles of the bottom of the mouth.

Confirmation can be found in photographs of patients at a young age, when the tissues were in good tonus, but the cervical angle was not acute due to the congenital position of the mouth’s diaphragm (the bottom of the mouth). In other words, the surgeon works with the individual initial data already available and the features of the anatomical structure of the individual patient’s face. The task is to approximate the state of the face contour to the face parameters at a young age, thereby achieving the effect of “rejuvenation”.

Facelift – MYTHS AND FACTS

The aging process can not be prevented and some patients resort to repeated surgical procedures aimed at improving the face. The reasons for the return are exactly the same as on the first visit to the surgeon.

I would like to dispel the myth that the process of facial aging after facelift is accelerating and a person is forced to constantly seek help in this context. Someone has only one operation and the effect persists for a very long time, while others can do the lift more than once.

It is important to know that facelift does not entail the need for repetition at all. Over the years, the corrected areas change, but already from the state that was obtained after the operation.

The timing of the appearance of indications for repeated facelift is absolutely individual. Someone will be able to intervene after 10 years, and someone after 3 years.

As a rule, this is associated with the rate of degeneration processes, which is individual and related to the genetics of a particular person.

Any way to mobilize, move and fix SMAS can be effective in every concrete clinical case, in the performance of a particular surgeon and takes place for existence, because “there is no such key that would open all the doors.” In other words, there is no universal method for correcting ptosis of facial tissues.

There are many facelift techniques with which you can achieve a good postoperative result. Its task is to simply choose the optimal one.
