Advertising Facelifts

Turn your TV on and chances are, there is an advertising facelift that is inviting you to improve and take off years from your face. One of the reasons for the many ads on facelift surgeries is the popularity of this type of procedure.

Add to the fact that everyone has an inherent fear of aging and wants to look perfect. Have picked up a magazine lately?

Dr. David Gilpin Facelift Surgery With Neck Lift

Going thru a facelift surgery helps in improving one’s self-image. Still, as with other procedures, risks are as many as the ads are a-plenty.

When going thru the facelift, patients are first put under anesthesia. The skin is then cut from the ear and then moves towards the hairline all the way to the side of the other ear.

The skin that is drooping is then tightened in order to create a smoother skin surface minus the wrinkles. Any scars seen could be immediately hidden by placing them under the ear and hairline.

A cosmetic surgery procedure is supported by one’s facial bone structure. The most excellent results then depends on how one’s bone structure is in good condition and on how thin one’s skin is.

Most women who do go through a facelift procedure is specially attracted to the look that is touted as “windswept” as the facial skin is tightly pulled even in the eye area.

Dr. Nicholas Sieveking Facial Lift Photo Result

The risks involved in facelift procedures are best avoided by quitting smoking and alcohol especially months before the actual surgery. Take note that smoking could affect the body’s ability to heal.

Recovery will also be affected if alcohol is taken as it could bring unnecessary side effects with the post-surgery medication. Cautiousness is necessary.

All in all, it is best to gather all information you need yourself and not solely trust on advertising facelift. In the end, you alone know what works best for your skin and what the best solution is that will resolve and respond to your unique needs and wants.

Facelift surgery in Nashville requires expertise not only in carrying out the surgery but in assessing what aspects of the face have aged and what procedures are appropriate to address facial ageing.

From full facelifts, to necklifts, to browlifts or even mini or mid facelifts, no single procedure or set of procedures is correct for every patient.
