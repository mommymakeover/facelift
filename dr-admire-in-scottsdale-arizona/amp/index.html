How many cc’s would I need to go up two cup sizes?
Breast implant sizing
Apr 25, 2020
Thanks for your question. My general rule is about 200cc per cup size. Therefore, increasing 2 cup sizes, this would be about a 400cc implant. This gives a general idea where to start regarding the implant needed. However, a proper exam, taking breast measurements into account and dimensional analysis is also needed. I would recommend an extra high, cohesive silicone implant for you to keep the breasts narrow and give you more anterior projection which will create some lift to your breasts.
Can I get a mini tuck or do I need a traditional TT?
Tummy Tuck questions
Apr 15, 2020
Thank you for your questions and pics. I recommend a full tummy tuck to get the best possible result. You have too much loose skin above and below the belly button - most if not all of that can be removed. Also would be able to remove or revise and lower the scar you have right above the belly button. Also it appears your stomach muscles would need to be tightened.
Breast implants and tummy tuck. Would things be more complicated if I have uneven breasts? (Ph
Breast questions
Apr 9, 2020
Thanks for your questions and pics. Having uneven breasts is not unusual - most women have some degree of asymmetry. My recommendation would be to do a lift (short scar technique) on the right w/ an implant, and just an implant on the left. This will provide the best symmetry, size and shape for both. The tummy tuck is relatively straightforward with both skin and fat removal and tightening of the vertical abdominal muscles.
Can you get both an areola reduction and a circumareolar lift (donut lift) at the same as a br
Breast augmentation question
Mar 23, 2020
Thank you for your questions. Both can be done safely at the same time. I'm not a huge fan of the donut lift however. It tends to cause excessive scarring around the areola, or scar spreading. The areolas tend to widen over time. And The lift isn't as powerful in tightening up the breast and improving shape and nipple position compared to a circumvertical lift.
Do I have tuberous breast deformity? What would be the best approach?
Breast augmentation question
Feb 20, 2020
Thank you for your questions and submitting your photos. I'm not 100% convinced you have tuberous breasts - if you do, it's a very mild form. My recommendation is to get the breast augmentation only. I think you will find it will give you better shape to both breasts, and can also improve your asymmetry with different sized implants. The lift scars to fix tuberous breasts are more extensive, and I find most patients with a mild form are very happy with just the augmentation...
Are my implants bottoming out or are they malpositioned?
Breast augmentation question
Feb 16, 2020
It does not appear that your implants are bottoming out, in fact just the opposite. Over time the implants can actually shift more upward as capsular contracture develops. It appears that your breasts have undergone changes over the last few years, which is very common from a variety of reasons. I recommend possibly a capsulectomy or capsulotomy, possible implant exchange (but if they are intact and only 5 years old, not necessary) and mastopexies. Best of luck.
Hello, Should I do a fat transfer to the face first or deep plane facelift first?
Facelift and fat transfer
Feb 9, 2020
It would be helpful to see some photos first. However, fat transfer and a facelift, regardless of type can typically be combined during the same surgery. More surgeons are combining the two at the same time for a full rejuvenation effect, especially around the perioral and periorbital areas, as well as to correct any asymmetry issues.
Can I get a breast augmentation if I am on medication?
Breast augmentation question
Feb 9, 2020
Ulcerative colitis or other inflammatory bowel disorders are not a contraindication to breast augmentation surgery. It sounds like you are healthy otherwise. Those medications do not inhibit healing or cause a risk for infection.
