Just started derma rolling with .5 on face, can I do my nose? And hands?
dermaroller
Nov 19, 2015
A derma roller can be used on any area of the body, however some areas have thinner skin than others and less fat. The nose is difficult to roll and can be somewhat uncomfortable, but it is safe. Consider treating the hands every few days until your skin gets more used to the roller and try pressing a little lighter on the thin skin of the hands.
Any suggestions for Tear troughs?
tear trough treatment
Nov 18, 2015
The tear trough hollowness that you have is in many cases a hereditary problem and can absolutely be treated with injectable fillers such as restylane or restylane silk. Because this is an " off label" use for injectable fillers make sure that you go to a very experienced injector who knows the anatomy of the area very well and does these types of treatments regularly such as a board certified facial plastic surgeon.
Can a facelift be performed after having a "life style lift" procedure?
face lift after lifestyle lift
Nov 18, 2015
Revision facelifts are quite common after having a "life style lift". Most facelifts should not require revision for 10 - 15 years, but in the case of the lifestyle lift, you may not have had a procedure that provided those lasting results. Secondary procedures are quite safe if performed by a board certified facial plastic surgeon or board certified general plastic surgeon.

Thermi tight burns
Nov 18, 2015
You should call the physician that performed your procedure and have him/her look at the area and advise about appropriate treatment. Burns can occur at too high of a setting in areas where the skin is thin. It will most likely resolve if treated properly. Best of Luck to you.
