Cost Of Facelift In Manhattan

Facelift in Manhattan is a plastic surgery, which can be safely called “aerobatics” of aesthetic surgery. The purpose of such intervention is the return of youth, freshness and resilience to the face. In fact, facelift is an attempt to turn back the clock.

Modern plastic surgery has a variety of different techniques that allow you to correct certain areas and achieve an excellent natural result.

Facelift is one of the most popular procedures among all kinds of aesthetic surgeries in Manhattan, New York City. The tissues lose their former elasticity, and the face literally falls down under the action of gravity, first small, and then deeper wrinkles appear on the skin, the fat layer also gradually disappears, and so the volume of the face decreases due the natural age-related changes.

A Good Rhytidectomy Leaves You Looking Like A Younger, More Natural

Thus, plastic facial surgery sets the following main goals:

  • The tightening of structures that have fallen due to age-related changes;
  • Wrinkle correction;
  • Correction of the volume of the face in those zones, where necessary.

The face of each person can be divided into four main areas:

  • forehead,
  • middle part,
  • Cheeks,
  • neck.

In each of these areas, natural age changes can proceed in different ways, respectively, and the methods for their correction should differ. That is why a facelift should be performed only by an experienced plastic surgeon after a competent intervention planning.

Forehead lift

Facelift in the forehead area is necessary to correct wrinkles in this area. With the help of plastic surgery, you can also raise the eyebrow line and adjust facial expression. Often, people resort to surgery, whose face looks too “harsh”, because the position of the eyebrows determines its expression to a greater extent. In a number of cases, a favorably performed surgery to lift the forehead zone allows for a slight adjustment of the cheek area.

A Lift Facial In Manhattan To Achieve Lasting, Natural Results

A specific technique, according to which the facelift will be carried out in the forehead area, is determined primarily by its height. Modern plastic surgeons in their practice apply three basic techniques:

Coronary forehead lift is used in cases where the forehead is located low. An arcuate incision is located on the scalp in this operation. Such correction, in addition to eliminating pronounced age-related changes, increases the height of the forehead;

Temporal forehead lift is suitable for patients with high forehead. When performing the operation with this method, the incision is located directly along the border of the forehead and the scalp. Temporal forehead lift allows you to correct the age changes, raise the eyebrow line and reduce the height of the forehead;

The endoscopic method is a gentle technique that does not involve the removal of excess skin.

Endoscopic facelift in the forehead area leaves virtually no scars. The disadvantages of this approach include the lowest efficiency in comparison with other techniques.

Midface lift

The middle part of the face is the site located between the corner of the mouth and the lower edge of the orbit. Correction of this zone may be required first of all, since age changes often appear here in the first place. Dark circles under the eyes, pronounced nasolabial wrinkles, insufficient volume of cheekbones are just some of the problems that arise with age in the area of the middle part of the face.

Proceeding from this, it is possible to single out several basic goals, for the achievement of which the lifting of the middle part of the face is performed:

  • Restoration of lost volume in the area of cheekbones;
  • Elimination of dark circles under the eyes;
  • Correction of nasolabial wrinkles.

Dr. Robert Ciardullo Plastic Surgery In Face Candidate

Modern plastic surgery has several techniques that help to lift the middle part of the face (subperiosteal technique, MACS technique, SMAS facelift). The choice of a specific technique depends on the individual characteristics of the person and the estimated amount of intervention.

If the main problem is flattened cheekbones, the doctor is most likely to recommend facelift using the subperiosteal technique. But it should be taken into account that this technique does not give a good effect if it is necessary to correct nasolabial folds, in contrast to, for example, the MACS technique, which also allows removing dark circles under the eyes.

The midface lift with the SMAS method is usually performed in addition to the tightening of the cheeks.

Mini-Face Lifts Minimize The Incision And The Recovery Time

Cheek lift

Facelift in the cheek area allows to correct the so-called “hamster” cheeks and the volume of the cheekbones. Most often, the operation is carried out according to the classical SMAS technique, which is the most effective in the case of the need for correcting the drooping cheeks and age wrinkles.

In the course of performing such an operation, the superficial muscular-aponeurotic system is strengthened, excess of the skin is removed while the facial expressions are fully preserved. There are many other techniques for the cheeks lifts, which are characterized by lower efficiency.

Such methods include the so-called mini facelift, which has recently been actively advertised. Experienced specialists assert, that all these operations are more suitable for patients of a relatively young age who do not require large-scale intervention.

Neck lift

As a rule, if a facelift is performed in the cheek zone, neck lift is carried out simultaneously. However, if desired, surgery in the neck area can be done separately using one of the following techniques:

  • the neck lift surgery with the removal of skin from the auricles is an outdated technique, which today is practically not used because of its inefficiency;
  • the SMAS method is the standard lateral lifting of the subcutaneous muscle tissue;
  • Feldman’s corset platysmaplasty improved the submental contour and jawline and eliminated platysma bands. It is this technique that allows surgeons to achieve the most aesthetic results and to ensure that the neck forms the right angle.
